Hari 27 / 30 Level B1 🌿 Alam & Lingkungan 🌍 Global Topic
Climate and Our Planet
📖 Membaca dan mengekspresikan pendapat tentang isu global 🔤 Expository + Opinion 📚 Modal Verbs + Cause & Effect
Bacaan

Our planet is extraordinary. It is the only place in the known universe where life exists — where oceans stretch for thousands of kilometres, where rainforests breathe oxygen into the air, and where animals as different as blue whales and tiny hummingbirds share the same world. But right now, our planet is sending us a warning. And we need to listen.

Climate change means that the Earth's average temperature is rising. This happens because of greenhouse gases — gases like carbon dioxide and methane — that trap heat in the atmosphere. Most of these gases come from burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas to make electricity, drive cars, and run factories. The more we burn, the more heat gets trapped, and the warmer our planet becomes.

The effects are already happening everywhere. Glaciers in the mountains — including the ones in Papua — are melting faster than ever before. Sea levels are rising, which threatens low-lying islands and coastal cities. Extreme weather events like floods, droughts, and super-typhoons are becoming more frequent and more powerful. In Indonesia, we already feel this — heavier rains, longer dry seasons, and damage to coral reefs in our seas.

I think about the forests of Kalimantan and Sumatra. These are some of the most biodiverse places on Earth — home to orangutans, Sumatran tigers, and thousands of species of plants that exist nowhere else. When we cut down these forests, we not only destroy habitats, we also release enormous amounts of stored carbon into the atmosphere. It is a double disaster.

But here is the part I find most hopeful: we know what causes this, and we know what to do. Renewable energy sources — solar panels, wind turbines, and hydroelectric power — can replace fossil fuels. Planting trees and protecting forests can absorb carbon. Changing the way we eat, travel, and use electricity in our daily lives can make a real difference.

Every single person has a role. Ayah Sigit uses energy wisely in his studio. Ibu Fety teaches her students about the environment. I try to turn off the lights when I leave a room. Small actions, done by millions of people, add up to something huge. As a young person in Indonesia, I believe my generation will be the one that solves this challenge — because we have no other choice, and because we care.

Grammar Fokus
✦ Modal Verbs untuk Solusi + Sebab-Akibat (Cause & Effect)
We should turn off the lights.
→ "should" = saran / hal yang sebaiknya dilakukan
We must reduce plastic use.
→ "must" = kewajiban / hal yang harus dilakukan
We could walk or cycle more.
→ "could" = kemungkinan / saran yang lebih lembut
Because of CO₂, the Earth is warming.
→ "because of" + noun phrase = menyatakan penyebab
Forests are cut, so animals lose their homes.
→ "so" = akibat langsung dari penyebab sebelumnya
If we plant trees, we can absorb more carbon.
→ "If... can/will" = solusi kondisional (jika... maka...)
💡 Cara bicara tentang masalah global di B1:
❌ "Climate change is bad."  →  ✅ "Because of greenhouse gases, the Earth's temperature is rising, which causes glaciers to melt."
❌ "We must help the Earth."  →  ✅ "If we switch to renewable energy, we could dramatically reduce carbon emissions."

Gunakan "which causes / which leads to / as a result" untuk rantai sebab-akibat yang terdengar lebih dewasa dan akademis!

Percakapan
Miss Rania — Sekolah Aslan
Good morning, everyone! Today we're going to talk about climate change. Aslan, what do you think is the biggest problem facing our planet right now?
Aslan
Good morning, Miss Rania! I believe climate change is the most urgent problem. Because of greenhouse gases from burning fossil fuels, the Earth's temperature is rising. This causes glaciers to melt, sea levels to rise, and weather to become more extreme. Even here in Bandung, we feel the difference — the rains are heavier and less predictable than before.
Miss Rania — Sekolah Aslan
Excellent answer! And what about Indonesia specifically — how does climate change affect us?
Aslan
Indonesia is especially vulnerable, Miss. Our coral reefs are being damaged by warmer ocean temperatures. The glaciers in Papua — which are very rare in Southeast Asia — are almost completely gone. And when forests in Kalimantan and Sumatra are cut down, it releases huge amounts of carbon AND destroys the habitat of orangutans and Sumatran tigers. It's a double disaster for us.
Miss Rania — Sekolah Aslan
That's very well said! So what can young people like you do about it?
Aslan
I think every small action matters. At home, I try to turn off lights, reduce plastic, and not waste food. But I also think the most important thing is to keep learning and talking about it — like right now in class! If my generation understands this problem deeply, we could be the ones who find the real solutions. I find it hopeful that renewable energy technology is getting better and cheaper every year. We have the tools — we just need the will to use them.
Miss Rania — Sekolah Aslan
Bravo, Aslan! That was a truly impressive answer. You spoke like an environmental advocate!
Aslan
Thank you, Miss! I just love this planet too much to stay quiet about it. 🌍

Tulis Surat Terbuka untuk Planetmu!
Hari ini, coba tulis satu paragraf "Dear Planet Earth" dalam bahasa Inggris. Ungkapkan:

✏️ Apa yang kamu kagumi dari bumi — gunakan adjective kaya: extraordinary, breathtaking, magnificent
✏️ Apa yang membuatmu khawatir — gunakan cause & effect: "Because of..., ...is happening"
✏️ Apa janjimu — gunakan modal verbs: "I will... / I promise to... / I believe we can..."

Contoh pembuka: "Dear Planet Earth, you are the most extraordinary home imaginable..."
