Chapter One
🌿 The Hidden Path
✅ Read! Aslan was walking home from school one afternoon. He always took the same road through the park. But today, something was different. Between two big oak trees, he saw a narrow path that he had never noticed before. The path was covered with soft green moss and tiny yellow flowers. The air around it smelled sweet, like rain and honey. Aslan stopped and looked carefully. At the start of the path, there was a small wooden sign. It said: "The Whispering Forest — gentle hearts welcome." Aslan felt a warm feeling in his chest. He took a deep breath and stepped onto the path. As soon as he did, the trees around him seemed to move closer, like they were giving him a hug.

Chapter Two
🌳 The Talking Trees
✅ Read! Aslan walked deeper into the forest. The trees were tall and very old. Their trunks were wide and covered with soft green moss. Suddenly, a low, gentle voice said, "Hello, little friend." Aslan spun around. There was nobody there — only the trees. Then a tall oak tree slowly turned one of its big branches toward him. "It was me," the tree said warmly. "My name is Elder Oak. We have been waiting for you." Aslan stared with wide eyes. "Trees can talk?" he asked. "We have always talked," Elder Oak replied. "But most people walk past too quickly to listen." Aslan felt a little ashamed. He had passed this park every day and never once stopped to listen. "I am sorry," he said softly. The tree smiled — and all the leaves rustled with happiness.

Chapter Three
🦉 The Wise Old Owl
✅ Read! Elder Oak pointed a branch toward a large, round hole high up in an old tree. Two big yellow eyes blinked slowly in the darkness. Then a plump owl flew down and landed on a branch near Aslan. "This is Professor Hoot," said Elder Oak. "He is the wisest creature in the forest." The owl tilted his round head and studied Aslan carefully. "What do you know about forests, young one?" the owl asked in a deep, slow voice. Aslan thought hard. "Trees give us air to breathe," he said. "And animals live here." "Good," said Professor Hoot. "But do you know that every tree, every insect, and every drop of rain is connected? If one part is hurt, all parts suffer." Aslan nodded slowly. He had never thought about it that way. The owl ruffled his feathers. "Come, there is more to learn," he said.

Chapter Four
🦌 The Lost Baby Deer
✅ Read! Professor Hoot led Aslan deeper into the forest. Then they heard a soft, sad sound. Behind a large fern, there was a tiny baby deer. Its big brown eyes were wet with tears. "I am lost," the little deer cried quietly. "I cannot find my mother." Aslan knelt down gently on the soft ground. He did not make a sudden move. He remembered what Elder Oak had told him — to be patient and quiet with animals. The baby deer came one step closer. Then another. It gently pressed its small nose against Aslan's hand. Aslan felt a warm glow in his heart. Professor Hoot spoke from above: "The forest animals trust only those who move slowly and speak gently." Elder Oak's branches swayed and pointed to the left. "Her mother is just beyond those trees," he said. Aslan slowly stood up and walked that way. The baby deer followed close behind. A moment later, they heard a happy sound — the mother deer had found her baby.

05
Chapter Five
🌟 The Forest's Secret
✅ Read! As the sun began to set, the forest glowed with golden and green light. Tiny fireflies came out and danced between the trees. Elder Oak called Aslan back to the hidden path. "Before you go," the old tree said, "we want to share our secret." All the trees and animals gathered around quietly. Professor Hoot spoke first. "The secret of the forest is simple. We do not fight. We do not compete. The big trees share water with the small ones. The animals protect each other. We all work together." Elder Oak then spoke softly. "The world outside is often noisy and busy. But if you stop and listen — to trees, to rivers, to birds — nature will always teach you something." Aslan looked around at the glowing trees and the small animals nearby. He felt deep peace inside. He made a promise to never throw rubbish in the forest, to plant a tree every year, and to always walk softly on the earth. The forest whispered together: "Thank you, Aslan. Come back soon." Aslan smiled and walked home. He knew that the forest was not just a place. It was a friend.
