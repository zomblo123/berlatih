The Starlight
Library

A story about a boy who discovers a magical library that only opens at night — and learns that books can take you anywhere. For Aslan (level: late beginner). 📚✨

Chapter One
🚪 The Hidden Door
✅ Read! Aslan was nine years old. He loved to read, but he lived in a small town with only one old library. One evening, while walking home from school, Aslan saw something strange. Between two tall trees, there was a wooden door that he had never seen before. The door was painted dark blue with silver stars. A small sign said: "Open at night. Readers welcome." Aslan was curious. He touched the cold metal handle. The door opened with a soft creak. Inside, he saw thousands of books. But the most amazing thing was the ceiling — it was a real night sky, full of twinkling stars.

Chapter Two
📖 The Talking Books
✅ Read! Aslan walked slowly between the tall bookshelves. Suddenly, a small red book fell to the floor. "Pick me up!" said a tiny voice. Aslan's eyes grew wide. He picked up the book and opened it. The pages were filled with pictures of faraway lands. "I am a traveling book," the book said. "When you read me, you can visit any place in the world." Aslan turned the page. He saw a picture of a snowy mountain. Suddenly, the library disappeared. Aslan felt cold wind on his face. He was standing on a real snowy mountain! He could see the white peaks and feel the fresh air. "This is incredible!" Aslan shouted.

03
Chapter Three
🌊 Under the Ocean
✅ Read! Aslan closed the mountain book and opened a blue one with fish on the cover. "I am the ocean book," it whispered. Aslan turned to the first page. In a blink, he was underwater! Colorful fish swam around him. A friendly dolphin came close and said hello with a click. Aslan saw a beautiful coral reef. The water was clear and warm. He could breathe easily, as if he had special gills. A sea turtle swam by slowly. "Welcome to the deep sea," the turtle seemed to say. Aslan felt happy and calm. He had always wanted to see the ocean, and now he was swimming inside it! After a while, he closed the book and returned to the library. His clothes were completely dry.

04
Chapter Four
🦁 The Jungle Adventure
✅ Read! The next book Aslan chose was thick and green. It had a picture of a jungle on the cover. "Be brave," the book said. "The jungle is full of surprises." Aslan opened the book and found himself in a green forest. Tall trees reached up to the sky. He heard birds singing and monkeys chattering. A colorful parrot landed on his shoulder. "Hello, reader!" the parrot squawked. Aslan walked carefully on the soft ground. He saw a tiger resting under a tree. The tiger opened one eye and looked at him, but did not move. Aslan felt a little scared, but also excited. He remembered what his mother always said: "Be respectful to animals, and they will respect you." Aslan stood still and watched the tiger quietly. After a few minutes, the tiger stood up and walked away into the bushes. Aslan smiled. He had passed the test.

05
Chapter Five
🌟 The Best Adventure
✅ Read! Aslan sat down on a soft chair in the library. He looked at all the books around him. There were books about space, dinosaurs, castles, and deserts. But then he saw a small brown book with no pictures on the cover. It looked very ordinary. He opened it. Inside, there was only one sentence: "The greatest adventure is sharing stories with people you love." Aslan thought about his little sister, Mia. She was only five and could not read big books yet. Aslan smiled. He took the small brown book and walked out of the library. The stars above were still twinkling. The next morning, Aslan went to the town library and asked the librarian, "Can I read stories to the young children?" The librarian smiled and said, "Of course! We have a story time every Saturday." Aslan felt proud. He had visited snowy mountains, the deep ocean, and a wild jungle. But the best adventure was teaching his sister and other children to love books. That night, Aslan looked at the place between the two tall trees. The magical door was gone. But he knew something important: the magic was not in the door. The magic was in the stories — and in his heart.
