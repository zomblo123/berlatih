Chapter One
🌠 The Falling Star
✅ Read! One clear night, Aslan was lying on the soft grass in his garden, looking up at the sky. The stars were bright and the moon was full and round. Aslan loved watching the sky. He could stay there for hours without getting bored. Then suddenly, he saw something streak across the sky — a bright line of gold and white, moving fast. It was a shooting star! But it did not disappear like the others. It came closer and closer. Aslan sat up quickly. The light got bigger. Then with a soft thud, something small landed in the flower bed near the garden wall. Aslan's heart beat fast. He stood up carefully, walked to the flower bed, and looked down. There, among the flowers, something was glowing — a warm, golden light, no bigger than his hand.

Chapter Two
🌟 Finding the Lost Star
✅ Read! Aslan knelt down and gently pushed the flower stems apart. There it was — a small five-pointed star, about the size of his palm. It was made of pure light, soft and warm like a candle. It had two tiny blinking eyes and a small curved mouth. The little star looked up at Aslan and made a soft, bell-like sound. "Hello," Aslan whispered. "Are you okay?" The star made the sound again — a little higher this time, like a question. "I think you fell from the sky," said Aslan. The star dimmed a little, as if it was sad. Then it looked up, toward the dark night sky far above, and blinked its tiny eyes slowly. Aslan understood right away. "Don't worry," he said softly. "I will help you get back home."

03
Chapter Three
💫 The Star's Wish
✅ Read! Aslan held the little star gently in his cupped hands. The star was warm and pulsed softly with light — like a tiny heartbeat. As Aslan walked, the star made little sounds — happy bell tones when they moved toward the open field, and worried tones when Aslan turned the wrong way. It was like a musical compass. Aslan climbed to the top of the hill at the edge of the town. From up there, the sky felt much closer. He sat on a flat rock and held the star up high. The star began to flicker — its light going bright, then dim, then bright again. Small sparks flew off it like tiny fireworks. A warm breeze came from somewhere above, as if the sky itself was reaching down. The star made a long, beautiful tone — like a song — and its light turned from gold to white to silver.

Chapter Four
🌌 Journey to the Sky
✅ Read! Something amazing happened next. The star rose slowly from Aslan's hands, floating upward by itself. It stopped at the height of his eyes and looked at him — really looked at him — for a long, quiet moment. Then it darted to the left, then right, then flew in a small circle around Aslan's head, leaving a ring of golden light in the air. Aslan laughed with surprise and delight. The star was saying thank you. Then it rose higher, faster. It left a long bright trail behind it, like a silver ribbon against the dark sky. Aslan watched, his mouth open and his eyes wide. The trail grew thinner and thinner until the star was just a tiny point of light — and then it settled into its place among the other stars. Aslan could still see it. It was the brightest one of all.

Chapter Five
✨ A Bright New Friend
✅ Read! Aslan sat on the rock for a long time, looking up at the star he had helped. It seemed to twinkle a little more than the others — a quick, warm blink just for him. He smiled. He felt something he had not expected: not sadness that the star was gone, but a deep, quiet happiness. On his way home, Aslan thought about what had happened. The star had not said a single word. It had no arms to give a hug. But it had shared its light, its warmth, and its little bell song with him. And in return, Aslan had given his time, his care, and his courage. He had helped someone who could not help themselves. That night, before he went to sleep, Aslan looked out of his bedroom window. The bright star was still there — shining steadily, as if it was keeping watch over him too. Aslan whispered, "Goodnight, little star." And it blinked once. Just for him.

You are a shining star, Aslan! ⭐

Just like the little star found its way home because of Aslan's kindness, your kindness and courage can light up the world for others. Every time you help someone — big or small — you leave a little trail of brightness behind. Remember: the most beautiful thing you can do is help someone find their way. 🌙✨
