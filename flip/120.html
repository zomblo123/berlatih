A Rainy Saturday
📖 Menceritakan pengalaman pribadi dengan detail yang lebih kaya 🔤 Descriptive Narrative 📚 Past Simple + Rich Adjectives
Bacaan

Last Saturday was one of those perfect, cosy Bandung days — the kind that only happens when the rain is heavy and the whole world feels quiet and warm inside. I woke up to the sound of raindrops tapping against my window. The sky outside was a deep, cloudy grey, and the air smelled wonderfully fresh.

I stretched, looked at the rain, and immediately decided: this was a day for staying home. I called Fathan and asked if he wanted to come over. He arrived twenty minutes later, completely soaked, holding his shoes above his head and laughing. "The umbrella broke!" he shouted from the door. Enin Ii shook her head and immediately brought him a dry towel and a warm cup of sweet tea.

We settled in the living room and played Minecraft for two hours. Fathan was determined to build the biggest castle we had ever made. It was enormous — with tall towers, a moat, and a drawbridge. I built the garden section. Little Alana sat beside us, watching with wide eyes, occasionally pointing at the screen and shouting "WOW!" It was adorable.

Around noon, the smell of Enin Ii's cooking drifted through the whole house. She had made steaming hot soto ayam — chicken soup with rice. On a cold rainy day, there is absolutely nothing better. We ate together — me, Fathan, Alana, Kakek Cecep, Enin Ii, and Ibu Fety. Ayah Sigit joined us from his studio, still wearing his headphones around his neck.

In the afternoon, Fathan and I challenged each other to read a short English story and then retell it. Ibu Fety — being a teacher — gave us feedback. She said my English had improved remarkably. I felt so proud.

By evening, the rain had slowed to a gentle drizzle. Fathan went home with a full stomach, dry clothes, and a big smile. I stood at the window for a while, watching the wet street glisten under the orange streetlights. It had been a simple day — but one of the happiest I can remember.

Grammar Fokus
✦ Descriptive Narrative — Membuat Cerita Terasa Hidup & Detail
I woke up to the sound of rain.
→ "woke up to the sound of" = bangun karena suara
He arrived completely soaked.
→ past verb + adverb + adjective (lebih deskriptif)
The smell drifted through the house.
→ pilih kata kerja yang spesifik: drifted (bukan just "went")
It had been a simple day — but one of the happiest.
→ kontras dengan "but" membuat kalimat lebih berkesan
The street glistened under the orange streetlights.
→ detail sensorik membuat pembaca "melihat" ceritamu
💡 Rahasia cerita yang bagus: gunakan detail sensorik — apa yang kamu lihat, dengar, cium, rasakan.
❌ "It rained."  →  ✅ "Raindrops tapped gently against the cold window."
❌ "We ate food."  →  ✅ "The steaming hot soto ayam warmed us from the inside."

Pilih kata kerja yang spesifik: drifted / glistened / settled / shook / stretched — bukan hanya "go / do / say"!
