Chapter One
🌠 The Falling Star
✅ Read! It was a quiet, cold night, and Aslan was sitting alone on the rooftop of his house, wrapped in a warm blanket. He loved to look at the stars. There were hundreds of them, scattered across the deep black sky like tiny diamonds. Aslan could name many of them. He had been learning about stars for months. Then, one star began to move. It was not the slow drift of a cloud — it moved fast, faster than any bird. A long golden tail followed behind it, blazing bright like fire. Aslan stood up. His blanket fell to the floor. The star fell lower and lower. It was not going to stop. It was coming straight down. Aslan held his breath. With a soft, warm flash of light, the star disappeared behind the hill at the edge of the village. And then everything was quiet again. Aslan grabbed his jacket and ran.

Chapter Two
💫 The Star Child
✅ Read! Aslan ran down the dark path, past the sleeping houses and through the tall grass. When he reached the hill, he stopped and looked around. There was a soft glow near a large stone. He walked closer, his heart pounding. Sitting on the ground, leaning against the stone, was a small child. But not a normal child. This child was made of pure light — soft gold and white, like a candle flame in the shape of a person. The child had big round eyes, like two tiny moons, and a gentle, trembling glow all over its body. Aslan knelt down slowly. He did not want to scare the small being. "Hello," he said softly. The star-child looked up. Its eyes were filled with silver light — and Aslan could see sadness in them. It opened its small, glowing mouth and said one word, barely a whisper: "Lost." Aslan felt his heart break a little. He sat down beside the star-child and said, "Don't worry. I'll help you."

Chapter Three
🌙 One Night Together
✅ Read! Aslan gently picked up the star-child. The little creature was warm and very light — like holding a lantern made of cloud. Together, they walked to the top of the hill. Along the way, something magical happened. Every step Aslan took, the star-child's glow grew a little brighter. It made soft, musical sounds — like someone humming a beautiful song. It touched Aslan's face with its tiny warm fingers and left a golden shimmer on his cheek. Aslan laughed, surprised. When they reached the top, Aslan sat down and held the star-child on his lap. They looked up at the sky together. The star-child pointed one glowing finger toward the darkness above and made a sad sound. It missed its home. "I know," said Aslan quietly. "The sky misses you too." He held it close and watched the stars above them, twinkling like old friends. Aslan felt, for the first time, that the universe was not so far away. It was right there, in his arms, warm and alive and glowing.

Chapter Four
🌌 The Long Way Home
✅ Read! Aslan noticed the sky was slowly getting lighter at the far edge — a thin purple line was beginning to appear. Dawn was coming. He knew, somehow, that the star-child had to return before the sun rose. Once the sunlight arrived, the little being of light might fade and be lost forever. Aslan stood up quickly. "It's time," he said. "You need to go home now." The star-child looked at him. Its glowing eyes searched his face, as if asking a question. Aslan gave it a brave, warm smile. He raised his arms and held the star-child as high as he could — reaching up toward the enormous, dark sky. "Fly," he whispered. "You know the way." For one second, nothing happened. Then the star-child began to glow brighter, and brighter, until Aslan had to close his eyes from the beautiful brilliance. He felt warmth on his hands, then nothing. When he opened his eyes, his hands were empty. But above him, one new star was rising — moving fast, ascending higher and higher, following the old familiar path back home.

Chapter Five
🌅 Goodbye, Little Star
✅ Read! Aslan stood on the hilltop alone. The wind was cold, but his heart felt tremendously warm. He watched until the new star was too small to see anymore — just a tiny point of perfect light, safely home among its family. Tears came to Aslan's eyes, but they were not tears of sadness. They were tears of something much deeper — a feeling that he had done something truly important. He had helped a living being find its way. He had let something beautiful go, not because he did not care, but because he cared so much. He understood now that real love is sometimes about letting go. The best things we hold are the ones we set free. As the first golden rays of morning touched the hilltop, Aslan took a long, deep breath. In his chest, he carried a secret treasure that no one else had — the memory of one magical night, one glowing friend, and one extraordinary goodbye. He looked up one last time and whispered, "Goodbye, little star. Shine bright up there." And in his heart, he knew — the star was shining just for him.

You are extraordinary, Aslan! ✨

Like Aslan, you have a heart big enough to help others — even when it means saying goodbye. True friendship is not always about holding on. Sometimes, the greatest act of love is to let someone go with all the warmth in your heart. Every beautiful thing you release leaves a glowing mark on the world — and on you. Keep being brave, kind, and full of wonder, Aslan. The stars are watching. 🌙💫
