Bacaan — Tulisan Penutup Aslan

Thirty days ago, I opened a blank page and began something I wasn't sure I could finish. I remember feeling nervous — English seemed so large, so full of rules and exceptions and words I had never heard before. But Ibu Fety said: "Just start, Aslan. You will grow as you go." She was right.

Looking back now, I can hardly believe how much has changed. In the first week, I wrote simple sentences about my family and my food. I struggled with verb tenses, forgot words mid-sentence, and sometimes read so slowly that a single paragraph took me ten minutes. But I kept going. Every single day, I kept going.

I have learned so many things — not just vocabulary and grammar, but how to think in English. I have written about rainy Saturdays in Bandung, about the street food I love, about the animals that fascinate me, about the climate and our planet, about making difficult decisions, and about the person I admire most in the world — my father. Each topic taught me something new, not just about the language, but about myself.

There were hard days. Days when the words would not come. Days when I wanted to skip and rest. But then I would think about Fathan, who was cheering me on. I would think about Alana, who would one day look up to me. I would think about Ayah Sigit, who never rushes and never gives up. And I would open the page again and try.

The biggest surprise of this journey? I actually enjoy English now. I enjoy finding the perfect word. I enjoy the way a good sentence sounds when it comes together. I enjoy reading a paragraph and understanding every single word without needing help. That feeling — that small, quiet victory — never gets old.

So this is not an ending. This is a beginning. I will keep reading. I will keep writing. I will keep making mistakes — and I will keep learning from them. Because that is what this journey has taught me most of all: the best way to grow is simply to never stop.

Thank you for reading my story. My name is Aslan, and this has been my English journey. It is far from over. In fact — it has just begun. 🌟

Grammar Fokus
✦ Present Perfect + Future Goals — Menceritakan Perjalanan & Mimpi ke Depan
I have learned so much this month.
→ Present Perfect: pengalaman yang dimulai dulu, masih terasa sekarang
I have grown more than I expected.
→ have/has + past participle → menekankan hasil, bukan kapan
Looking back, I can hardly believe how far I've come.
→ "Looking back" = membuka refleksi dengan gaya; ekspresi dewasa
I will keep reading and writing every day.
→ "will keep + -ing" = komitmen untuk terus melakukan sesuatu
This is not an ending — it has just begun.
→ kontras yang kuat: penutup yang sebenarnya membuka sesuatu baru
💡 Cara menulis refleksi yang kuat:
✏️ Mulai dengan kondisi awal: "Thirty days ago, I was..."
✏️ Ceritakan prosesnya: "There were hard days, but I kept going."
✏️ Tunjukkan perubahan: "I have learned, I have grown..."
✏️ Tutup dengan ke depan: "I will keep... This is just the beginning."

Ingat: Refleksi terbaik bukan hanya "saya sudah belajar banyak" — tapi ceritakan bagaimana rasanya, apa yang sulit, dan apa yang berubah di dalam dirimu. 🌟
