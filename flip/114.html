Going to the Doctor
📖 Kosakata kesehatan, gejala sakit & dialog di klinik 🔤 I have a headache / I feel... 📚 Body Parts & Symptoms
Bacaan

Hello! My name is Aslan. Last week something happened that I will not forget. I woke up on Tuesday morning and I did not feel well at all. My head was aching, my throat was sore, and I felt very tired. My mother Fety looked at me and said, "Aslan, you look pale. We need to go to the doctor today."

At the clinic, a kind nurse asked me to wait and then measured my temperature. She said, "You have a slight fever — 37.8 degrees." Then the doctor called my name. His name was dr. Hendra. He shook my hand and smiled. "So, Aslan, how are you feeling today?" he asked.

I tried my best to explain in English — because I am practising! I said, "I have a headache and a sore throat. I also feel very tired." dr. Hendra nodded and examined my throat and chest. He said, "You have a mild infection. Nothing serious — but you need rest and medicine."

He wrote a prescription and said, "Take this tablet three times a day — after breakfast, after lunch, and after dinner. And drink plenty of water." My mother Fety wrote everything down carefully. She always does that so we never forget the instructions.

Two days later I felt much better. My fever was gone and my throat was no longer sore. I learned something important: being able to describe your symptoms clearly to a doctor is a very useful life skill — especially in English!

Contoh Percakapan di Klinik
🩺
Dialog 1 — Aslan diperiksa oleh dr. Hendra
Klinik Sehat Bersama · Selasa pagi · Ruang periksa
dr. Hendra
Good morning, Aslan! Please sit down. How are you feeling today?
Aslan
Good morning, Doctor. I don't feel well. I have a headache and a sore throat. I also feel very tired.
Dr Hendra
I see. How long have you felt like this?
Aslan
Since this morning. When I woke up I felt very bad. I also have a fever, I think — the nurse said 37.8 degrees.
dr. Hendra
Let me check your throat. Open your mouth, please, and say "Aahh".
Aslan
Aahh... Is it bad, Doctor?
dr. Hendra
It is a little red — a mild infection. Do you have a cough or a runny nose?
Aslan
Yes, I have a slight cough — but not a runny nose. My chest also feels a little tight.
dr. Hendra
Okay. I will give you some medicine. Take one tablet three times a day after meals. And please rest at home for two days. Drink lots of water!
Aslan
Thank you, Doctor. Is it serious?
dr. Hendra
No, nothing serious. You will feel better in two or three days. Come back if you don't improve. Take care, Aslan!
💊
Dialog 2 — Ibu Fety bertanya pada perawat tentang obat
Di meja resepsionis klinik · Setelah konsultasi selesai
Ibu Fety
Excuse me. Could you explain the prescription, please? I want to make sure I understand.
Nurse
Of course! Aslan needs to take one tablet three times a day — after breakfast, after lunch, and after dinner. Always after eating, not before.
Ibu Fety
How many days does he need to take the medicine?
Nurse
For five days. Make sure he finishes all the medicine — even if he feels better after two days!
Ibu Fety
Are there any side effects I should know about?
Nurse
He might feel a little sleepy — that is normal. Make sure he drinks plenty of water every day. If there is a rash or he feels worse, come back immediately.
Ibu Fety
Thank you so much. You have been very helpful!
Nurse
You're welcome! Get well soon, Aslan! 😊

Do's & Don'ts saat ke Dokter dalam Bahasa Inggris
✅ DO — Lakukan ini!
✓ Mulai dengan: "I don't feel well."
✓ Sebutkan gejala satu per satu dengan jelas
✓ Sebutkan sudah berapa lama: "since yesterday"
✓ Tanya jika tidak mengerti: "Could you repeat that?"
✓ Tanya efek samping obat: "Are there any side effects?"
✓ Ucapkan terima kasih di akhir: "Thank you, Doctor."
❌ DON'T — Hindari ini!
✗ Jangan hanya bilang "I am sick" — jelaskan gejalanya!
✗ Jangan diam jika tidak mengerti instruksi dokter
✗ Jangan berhenti minum obat sebelum habis
✗ Jangan bilang "My head is aching" — pakai "I have a headache"
✗ Jangan lupa tanya: "How many times a day?"
✗ Jangan malu berbicara — dokter siap membantu!

Grammar Fokus
✦ Dua pola utama untuk menyampaikan gejala sakit
I have a headache.
→ I have + a/an + nama sakit → untuk gejala yang bisa "dimiliki"
I have a fever / a cough.
→ I have a fever · a cough · a runny nose · a sore throat
I feel dizzy / tired / sick.
→ I feel + adjective → untuk perasaan atau kondisi tubuh
My chest feels tight.
→ My + bagian tubuh + feels/hurts → untuk rasa sakit di bagian tertentu
Since this morning / yesterday.
→ Since + waktu → menyampaikan sudah berapa lama sakit
Take one tablet three times a day.
→ Pola instruksi dokter: Take + jumlah + times a day / week
💡 Ingat perbedaan penting:

✅ "I have a headache." — benar (gunakan HAVE + a + sakit)
❌ "My head is aching." — terlalu rumit untuk A2, hindari dulu

✅ "I feel dizzy." — benar (FEEL + kata sifat)
❌ "I am feeling dizzy." — tidak salah, tapi "I feel" lebih umum dan natural

🔑 Pola "How long have you felt like this?" dipakai dokter untuk menanyakan durasi gejala. Jawab dengan: "Since [waktu]" atau "For [durasi]".
Percakapan
Guru — Miss Dewi
Aslan, I heard you went to the doctor last week. How are you feeling now?
Aslan
I feel much better now, Miss! Thank you for asking. I had a headache and a sore throat, and also a slight fever. The doctor said it was a mild infection — nothing serious!
Guru — Miss Dewi
That's great to hear! Did you speak English with the doctor?
Aslan
Yes, Miss! I tried to explain my symptoms in English. I said "I have a headache and I have a sore throat. I also feel very tired." The doctor was dr. Hendra — he was very kind and patient with me. He even smiled when I spoke in English!
Guru — Miss Dewi
Excellent! What did the doctor tell you to do?
Aslan
He said "Take one tablet three times a day — after breakfast, after lunch, and after dinner." And he told me to drink plenty of water and rest at home for two days. My mother Fety wrote everything down so we would not forget. The nurse was also very helpful — she explained the side effects too!
Guru — Miss Dewi
Perfect! What is the most useful phrase you learned from this experience?
Aslan
"I have a headache" and "I feel very tired" — those two patterns can describe almost any symptom! I also learned to say "Since this morning" when the doctor asked how long I felt sick. Ayah Sigit said I was very brave for talking to the doctor in English all by myself! 😊
