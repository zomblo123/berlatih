Chapter One
🌱 The Golden Seed
✅ Read! One quiet morning, Aslan was helping his grandfather clean the old garden shed. The shed was full of dusty boxes and forgotten tools. Aslan opened a small wooden box in the corner and found something very strange. Inside, there was a single seed. It was the size of a large marble, and it glowed with a soft, warm, golden light. Aslan picked it up carefully. The seed felt warm in his hand, like a tiny sun. His grandfather walked over and looked at the seed with wide eyes. "I have not seen this in many, many years," the old man said in a quiet voice. "That is the last golden seed in the world, Aslan. Guard it well." Aslan stared at the small glowing object in his hand. He could feel a gentle warmth moving up his fingers and into his heart. Something told him that this seed was very, very special.

Chapter Two
🌳 The Wise Old Tree
✅ Read! Aslan walked to the big old oak tree at the end of the garden. He often sat under this tree when he needed to think. He placed the golden seed gently on the ground in front of him and looked at it carefully. Suddenly, the oak tree above him made a slow, deep sound, like the wind moving through its oldest branches. Then a warm voice seemed to come from the trunk itself. "You have found it at last," the tree said softly. Aslan was not afraid. He felt calm and still. "What is this seed?" he asked. The old tree was quiet for a moment. Then it spoke again. "That seed can grow into the most magical tree the world has ever known. Its fruit can feed a hundred people. Its shade can cool a whole village. Its flowers bloom in every colour of the rainbow." Aslan held his breath and looked down at the tiny glowing seed. It was hard to believe that something so small could hold so much power.

Chapter Three
⚖️ The Decision
✅ Read! That night, Aslan lay in bed and stared at the ceiling. The golden seed sat on his bedside table, still glowing softly in the dark. He had a choice to make. He could plant the seed in his own backyard. Then the magical tree would be his alone. He could eat its fruit every day, sit under its beautiful shade, and share it only with the people he chose. It sounded wonderful. But then Aslan thought about his neighbours. Old Mrs Pinar next door, who had no garden. The children at the village school who had no big trees to play under. The tired farmers who walked long roads in the hot sun with no shelter. Aslan sat up in bed. His heart felt heavy and his thoughts were loud. He asked himself a quiet but important question: "Who does this seed really belong to?" He looked at the seed. It seemed to glow a little brighter, as if it understood his question. Aslan took a slow, deep breath and closed his eyes. By morning, he knew exactly what he had to do.

Chapter Four
🤲 Planting the Seed
✅ Read! The next morning, Aslan got up early. He held the golden seed carefully in both hands and walked to the centre of the village square. It was the widest, most open space in the whole village — the place where everyone came to meet, to rest, and to celebrate together. A few early risers watched him with curious eyes. Old Mrs Pinar came out of her house. "What are you doing, Aslan?" she asked. "I am planting something for everyone," Aslan replied with a smile. He knelt down on the soft earth and used his hands to dig a small, neat hole. He placed the seed inside very gently, as if it were something precious and alive — which, of course, it was. Then he covered it carefully with dark, rich soil and pressed the earth down firmly with both palms. He poured a little water from a small clay jar. For a moment, nothing happened. Then, from deep below the ground, a soft golden light began to spread through the soil, reaching outward like tiny glowing roots. The crowd around Aslan grew quiet. Even the birds seemed to stop singing. Everyone held their breath and watched.

Chapter Five
🌟 The Forest of Gold
✅ Read! By the next morning, a miracle had happened. Where the seed had been planted, a tall and magnificent tree now stood. Its trunk was thick and strong, the colour of warm amber. Its branches reached wide and high, like arms offering a great hug to the sky. Its leaves were the deepest, richest green, and between them hung hundreds of golden fruits that glowed like small lanterns. The whole tree seemed to hum with quiet, gentle energy. The village people gathered in amazement. Children climbed the wide lower branches and laughed with joy. Old Mrs Pinar sat in the cool shade and said it was the most peaceful place she had ever rested. The tired farmers ate the golden fruit and said their strength came back immediately. The tree grew more fruit faster than anyone could eat, so the people shared it with villages nearby. And then something even more magical happened. Seeds from the golden tree began to fall gently to the ground. Everywhere they landed, new trees began to grow. Within a year, the whole area had become a forest of gold. Aslan stood at the edge of the forest and smiled. He had given something up, yes. But what he received in return was far greater — a whole world made more beautiful by one small, brave, and generous act.
