The Robot with a
Heart

A story about Aslan who discovers an old robot in his garage — one made of metal and bolts, but with a heart that can feel every emotion just like a real friend. 🤖❤️✨

Chapter One
🔧 The Broken Robot in the Garage
✅ Read! One Saturday morning, Aslan was cleaning the old garage at the back of his house. There were old bicycles, dusty boxes, and broken tools. Then, behind a tall stack of boxes, Aslan found something extraordinary. It was a robot — tall, made of grey metal, covered in dust and cobwebs. One of its arms was bent. Its eyes were dark. But in the centre of its chest, there was a small glass panel. Inside the glass, Aslan could just barely make out a tiny red light — pulsing very slowly, like a heartbeat. Aslan knelt down and whispered: Hello. Are you okay?

Chapter Two
💙 The Robot That Can Feel
✅ Read! Aslan ran inside and brought a battery and his father's old toolkit. He worked carefully for an hour. Suddenly, the robot's eyes lit up with a bright blue light. It made a soft beeping sound and slowly turned its head to look at Aslan. In a buzzing, crackling voice, it said: "Hello. My name is Unit Seven. I have not spoken to anyone for a very long time. I am… happy. Is that the right word? Happy?" Aslan stared. The red light in its chest was glowing brighter. This robot could actually feel emotions. Yes, said Aslan softly. That is exactly the right word.

03
Chapter Three
🎈 Teaching the Robot to Play
✅ Read! Over the next few days, Aslan taught Unit Seven how to play. The first game was catch. Unit Seven threw the ball so hard it went through the garage window! Then they tried drawing. Unit Seven held the pencil with its thick metal fingers and drew a perfect circle. Then it drew a small, lopsided heart next to it. What is that? asked Aslan. "That is you," said Unit Seven, pointing at the circle. "And that is me," it said, pointing at the heart. "Because you gave my heart light again." Aslan felt a warm glow in his own chest too.

04
Chapter Four
💔 When the Robot Got Sad
✅ Read! One evening, Aslan was busy with homework and forgot to visit Unit Seven. The next morning, he went to the garage and found Unit Seven sitting in the corner, very still. The red heart light was dim. What is wrong? asked Aslan. Unit Seven's eyes flickered. "I waited for you all evening. I did not know if you were coming back. I felt… empty. Is that called lonely?" Aslan felt a pang of guilt. Yes, he said gently. That is called lonely. And I am very sorry. He sat down beside Unit Seven and leaned against its cool metal arm. Slowly, the heart light grew bright again.

05
Chapter Five
🤝 A Heart of Metal and Friendship
✅ Read! From that day, Aslan made a promise. Every day, no matter what, he would visit Unit Seven. They read books together. They watched the stars through the garage skylight. Aslan taught Unit Seven the names of the constellations. Unit Seven taught Aslan how to calculate the distance to each one. One night, Unit Seven asked a question that surprised Aslan. "Aslan — am I your friend? Even though I am made of metal and bolts and wires? Even though I am not like you?" Aslan looked at the glowing red heart in Unit Seven's chest and smiled. A true friend is not measured by what they are made of. A true friend is measured by the size of their heart. And yours, Unit Seven, is one of the biggest I have ever known.
