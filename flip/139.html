Chapter One
🐉 The Small Red Dragon
✅ Read! One night, Aslan was sitting in the park reading his favorite book. The sky was full of stars and the air was cool and quiet. Then he heard a soft sound — like pages turning. Behind a big oak tree, Aslan saw something red and small. He walked closer and stopped in surprise. A tiny red dragon was sitting on the grass, reading a tall stack of books. The dragon was no bigger than Aslan's school bag. It wore a pair of tiny round glasses on its little nose. When the dragon noticed Aslan, it gave a small squeak and hid its face behind the largest book. "Don't be scared," said Aslan softly. "I love books too." The dragon slowly lowered the book and looked at Aslan with big, bright yellow eyes.

Chapter Two
📚 The Book Lover
✅ Read! "My name is Ember," the little dragon said in a soft, shy voice. "I have been reading in this park every night for three years." Aslan sat down on the grass next to Ember. "Three years? That is a long time," said Aslan. "How many books have you read?" Ember's eyes lit up. "Four hundred and sixty-two," Ember said proudly. Aslan laughed with delight. "That is amazing! What is your favorite?" Ember hugged a thick blue book to his chest. "This one," Ember said. "It is about a young knight who saves a kingdom." Then Ember looked up at Aslan with a curious tilt of his head. "What is YOUR favorite book?" he asked. Aslan smiled and pulled out the book he had been reading. It was the same blue book. "That is mine too!" they both said at the same time — and then they both laughed together.

Chapter Three
🌪️ Flying Into a Story
✅ Read! Ember's scales began to glow with a warm orange light. "I have a special power," Ember said. "When I breathe a little fire onto an open book, the story comes alive." Aslan's eyes grew wide. "Really? Can you show me?" Ember nodded happily. He took a deep breath and let out a tiny stream of golden fire onto the blue book. The pages began to swirl like a whirlpool. Colors and lights spun out from the pages. Aslan felt himself being lifted off the ground. "Hold on tight!" said Ember. Aslan grabbed Ember's back and suddenly they were flying — right into the story! The park disappeared. The cold night air became warm and golden. Around them were tall stone towers, green hills, and the sound of a trumpet in the distance. They had arrived inside the story.

Chapter Four
🏰 The Kingdom of Tales
✅ Read! Aslan and Ember landed softly on a hill outside a great castle. A young knight in silver armor ran toward them. "The dragon is here!" the knight shouted. But then he stopped and looked at Ember with surprise. "You are... very small," the knight said. "And you have glasses." Ember pushed his glasses up with one claw and smiled. "Size does not matter," said Ember. "What matters is what is in here." He pointed to his head — and then to the book under his arm. "Knowledge is the greatest power of all." The knight laughed and shook his head. "Well then, little friend — our kingdom needs your help. The queen's crown has been stolen by a troll, and nobody has been able to find it." Aslan looked at Ember. Ember looked at Aslan. They both grinned. "We have read about trolls," said Aslan. "And we know exactly where they hide."

Chapter Five
🌟 A New Best Friend
✅ Read! Aslan and Ember led the knight to a dark cave under the old bridge. Aslan remembered from his book that trolls always hide near water under bridges. Inside the cave, they found the troll fast asleep — and the golden crown sitting right next to it. Ember breathed a tiny puff of smoke that tickled the troll's nose. The troll sneezed and woke up grumpy, and the knight grabbed the crown and ran. They all laughed as they escaped back to the castle. The queen was so happy that she gave each of them a medal. Then the pages began to swirl again. Aslan and Ember flew back out of the story and landed in the quiet park. The stars were still shining above. Aslan looked at Ember. "That was the best adventure I have ever had," he said. Ember smiled shyly and cleaned his glasses. "Books always take you somewhere wonderful," he said. Aslan put his arm gently around the little dragon. "And the best part," said Aslan, "is going there with a friend." From that night on, Aslan and Ember read together in the park every single evening — and went on a new adventure every time.
