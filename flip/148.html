The Mirror of
Dreams

A story about Aslan who discovers a magical mirror in an old attic — and steps through it into a world where every dream he has ever had becomes beautifully real. 🪞✨🌙

Chapter One
🏚️ The Old Mirror in the Attic
✅ Read! One rainy afternoon, Aslan was exploring the attic of his grandmother's house. The attic was full of old boxes, dusty books, and forgotten things. Aslan liked going up there because it felt like a world of its own. He pushed aside a heavy old curtain and found himself in a small space he had never seen before. Against the far wall stood a tall mirror. It had a beautiful golden frame decorated with stars and moons. Dust covered the glass, but when Aslan wiped it with his sleeve, the mirror did not show his reflection. Instead, the glass glowed with soft purple and silver light, like the inside of a cloud at night. Aslan's breath caught in his throat. He leaned closer and touched the cool glass with one finger.

Chapter Two
🌀 Stepping Into the Mirror
✅ Read! The moment Aslan's finger touched the glass, the mirror's surface became soft like water. The purple light grew brighter and swirled in gentle circles. A warm, sweet breeze came out from the glass, and Aslan heard very faint music — like a lullaby he had heard long ago but could not quite remember. Without thinking, he pressed his whole hand against the mirror. The glass parted like a curtain of light. Aslan felt a gentle pull. He took one brave step forward — and walked straight through. There was a rush of warm air, a flash of purple and gold, and then silence. Aslan opened his eyes. He was standing in a wide, open land under a sky full of enormous glittering stars. The air smelled of flowers and honey. Everything felt soft and full of light.

03
Chapter Three
🌈 Living the Dream
✅ Read! Aslan walked forward and discovered that this world was built from his own dreams. Everything he had ever wished for was here. There was a tall castle with towers that reached into the clouds — just like the one he used to draw in his notebook. A river of glowing blue water ran beside it. On the riverbank, a magnificent white horse was drinking from the water. The horse looked up at Aslan and trotted over to him. "Hello," said Aslan carefully. The horse nodded its beautiful head as if it understood. Aslan climbed onto the horse's back and they galloped across the open fields. The wind rushed through Aslan's hair. He laughed loudly — a free, joyful laugh that he had never heard from himself before.

04
Chapter Four
💭 When Dreams Become Real
✅ Read! After exploring for what felt like hours, Aslan sat on the hilltop and looked out at the dream world. He could see the castle, the glowing river, mountains covered in soft purple clouds, and a sky full of stars even though it was daytime. It was perfect. But then, a gentle voice seemed to come from everywhere at once. "This world will stay as long as you believe," the voice said. Aslan looked around but saw nobody. "What do you mean?" he asked quietly. "Every dream in here," the voice continued, "began as a real thought in your real mind. What you imagine, you can build. What you practice, you can achieve. The mirror shows you what is already inside you." Aslan felt a shiver of understanding run through him. These were not just dreams. These were possibilities.

05
Chapter Five
🌟 The Most Beautiful Dream
✅ Read! Aslan stood up and took one long, last look at the dream world. The castle, the horse, the glowing river — all of it was beautiful. But he also felt something tugging at his heart. He missed his grandmother. He missed the smell of her cooking and the sound of her humming in the kitchen. He missed his room, his books, and even his homework. He missed the real world. Aslan walked back to where the mirror waited, glowing softly. He stepped through it again. There was the same rush of warm air — and then he was back in the dusty attic. The mirror returned to its quiet silver glow. Aslan sat down on an old wooden box and smiled. He now understood something important. Dreams were beautiful and necessary. They gave him energy and direction. But the real world — with its real people and real effort — was where dreams actually came true. He took out his notebook and began to write: the name of the book he wanted to write one day, the skills he wanted to learn, the places he wanted to visit. Dream by dream, step by step — Aslan was going to build his castle for real.

You are a dreamer AND a doer, Aslan! 🌟

Just like Aslan discovered, the most magical mirror is your own mind. Every big dream you have is a clue about what you can truly become. But dreams need action — just like seeds need water. Remember: dare to dream big, and then take one small step toward it every single day. ✨🏰

💪 Today's Challenge: Take out a notebook and draw or write your THREE biggest dreams. Next to each dream, write ONE real step you can take this week to get closer to it. Your mirror of dreams starts right there! 🪞📝
