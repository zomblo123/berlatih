The Secret Garden Door

A story about a boy named Aslan who finds a mysterious old door behind his house — and steps into a magical garden where flowers speak, animals sing, and time feels like a dream. For Aslan (level: late beginner). 🌸✨

Chapter One
🚪 The Old Wooden Door
✅ Read! Aslan was eight years old. He lived in a house with a big garden. One Saturday morning, Aslan was playing near the old stone wall at the back of the garden. He had never walked to that part before. The grass there was very tall and green. Suddenly, he saw something hidden behind the tall grass — a small wooden door in the wall. The door was very old. It had beautiful flowers carved on it. Green vines grew all around it. A tiny golden lock hung on the front. Aslan looked carefully. There was no key, but when he touched the door with one finger, the lock clicked open all by itself. Aslan took a deep breath. He pushed the door and stepped through.

Chapter Two
🌸 Inside the Secret Garden
✅ Read! Aslan stepped inside and stopped. He could not believe his eyes. There was a beautiful garden full of colors. The flowers were red, pink, yellow, and purple. Some of them glowed softly like little lamps. The trees were tall and their leaves were silver and gold. Soft green grass covered the ground like a carpet. The sky above was a deep purple color with tiny stars, even though it was morning. Butterflies with bright wings flew slowly between the flowers. The air smelled sweet and fresh, like honey and rain. Aslan walked slowly forward, his mouth open in wonder. He felt calm and happy. It was the most beautiful place he had ever seen. He whispered to himself, "This is real."

Chapter Three
🌺 The Singing Flowers
✅ Read! Aslan walked deeper into the garden. He heard a soft sound — like music. He stopped and listened. It was coming from the flowers! A tall pink flower turned its head and sang, "Hello, Aslan! We are happy you came!" Aslan's eyes went wide. He looked at the other flowers. A small yellow flower said, "We have been waiting for a kind visitor." A purple flower added, "Only children with good hearts can open our door." Aslan felt warm inside. "You know my name?" he asked. "Of course," said the pink flower with a little giggle. "The garden knows everything." Aslan sat down among the flowers. He listened to their sweet songs. He felt like he was in a dream. But the flowers felt real. Their petals were soft and warm. "I will take care of you," Aslan said gently.

04
Chapter Four
💧 The Magical Fountain
✅ Read! Aslan followed a small path through the garden. At the center, he found a beautiful stone fountain. The water in it was sparkling blue and silver, like liquid light. Around the fountain, small birds sat singing. A white rabbit sat nearby, tapping its foot to the music. Aslan dipped one finger into the water. It was cool and magical — his finger began to glow softly for a moment! A small blue bird flew to his shoulder and sang a little melody. "If you drink from the fountain," the rabbit said in a quiet voice, "you will remember this garden forever." Aslan bent down and took one sip of the cool water. It tasted like strawberries and morning dew. He smiled. He knew he would never forget this wonderful place. The birds all sang louder, as if they were celebrating.

05
Chapter Five
🌟 The Way Back Home
✅ Read! After a long while, the pink flower spoke again. "Aslan, the sun is moving. It is time to go home." Aslan looked up. The sky was slowly changing to orange and pink. He did not want to leave, but he knew his mother would worry. He said goodbye to each flower. He gently touched the soft petals. He waved to the birds and the white rabbit. "Will I be able to come back?" he asked sadly. "Yes," said the flowers together. "But only if you continue to be curious and kind." Aslan walked back to the wooden door and stepped through. The door closed softly behind him. He stood again in his own garden. Everything looked the same — but Aslan felt different. He felt braver and more grateful. He looked at the flowers and trees in his own garden and saw them differently now. He thought, "I must take care of the nature around me. Every flower, every tree is a small wonder." That evening, Aslan helped his mother water the garden — and he did it with a big, happy smile.
