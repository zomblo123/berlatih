The Brave Little
Lantern

A story about Aslan who finds a small talking lantern that is afraid of the dark — and together they learn that the bravest light is the one that shines even when it is most afraid. 🏮🌙✨

Chapter One
🏚️ The Old Lantern in the Attic
✅ Read! One rainy afternoon, Aslan was helping his grandmother tidy up the old attic. The attic smelled of wood and dust, and every corner was full of forgotten things. Aslan moved boxes, folded old blankets, and swept the dusty floor. Then, behind a tall stack of boxes, he found something small and round. It was an old brass lantern. The lantern was no bigger than Aslan's two hands put together. Its metal body was a warm golden color, and its tiny glass window was perfectly clear. Aslan picked it up carefully. The moment his fingers touched the handle, a small flame appeared inside the lantern. The flame was soft and warm, like a friendly fire. Aslan stared at it, surprised. Then he heard something even more surprising — a tiny, high voice coming from inside the lantern. "Oh! Hello there," said the voice. "I have been waiting a very long time for someone kind to find me."

Chapter Two
😨 The Lantern That Was Afraid
✅ Read! Aslan brought the lantern to his room and sat on the bed. "What is your name?" Aslan asked. The little voice replied, "My name is Lumi." Aslan smiled. "That is a beautiful name. Why were you hiding in the attic?" Lumi's flame flickered and grew very small. "Because I am afraid," Lumi said in a quiet voice. "Afraid of what?" asked Aslan, leaning closer. "Of the dark," Lumi whispered. Aslan blinked. He could not believe it. A lantern — a creature made of light — was afraid of the dark. "But you ARE the light," said Aslan gently. "I know," said Lumi sadly. "But every time I go outside at night and see how big and dark the world is, my flame becomes very small and I feel like I cannot shine at all. I have been hiding for so long that I almost forgot I could glow." Aslan looked at the little lantern with great kindness. He understood that feeling very well.

Chapter Three
🌲 Into the Dark Forest
✅ Read! That same evening, Aslan's grandmother called him. "Aslan, I need you to walk down the forest path and bring this basket to old Mr. Yusuf's cottage. He is sick and needs food." The forest path was long and there were no streetlights. It would be very dark. Aslan looked at Lumi. "Do you want to come with me?" he asked. Lumi's flame trembled. "The forest at night… it is very dark," Lumi said nervously. "I know," said Aslan. "That is exactly why I need you." There was a long silence. Then Lumi said in a small but determined voice, "Okay. I will try." Aslan smiled warmly and picked up the lantern and the basket. They stepped out of the house together into the dark, cool night. The moment they left the light of the house, the darkness surrounded them like a thick, soft blanket. Lumi's flame trembled wildly. "I am scared," Lumi said. "That is okay," said Aslan calmly. "I am right here. Take one step at a time."

Chapter Four
💡 Facing the Darkness
✅ Read! As Aslan and Lumi walked deeper into the forest, the trees grew tall and the path became hard to see. Lumi's light flickered and trembled with every strange sound — the hoot of an owl, the rustle of leaves, the crack of a twig. "I cannot do this," Lumi cried. "What if I go out? What if my flame disappears and you are left all alone?" Aslan stopped walking. He held the lantern up gently and looked into Lumi's little glowing face. "Lumi," he said quietly, "look around. Look at what your light is doing right now, even while you are afraid." Lumi looked. The warm circle of golden light reached the mossy path, the tree trunks, the ferns, and even a family of fireflies sitting on a low branch. "You are lighting the way," said Aslan. "You ARE shining — even while you are trembling. That is what courage really means. Not the absence of fear — but shining anyway." Lumi went quiet for a long moment. Then, very slowly, Lumi's flame grew a little bigger. And a little bigger. And a little bigger still.

Chapter Five
🌟 The Bravest Light
✅ Read! Aslan and Lumi reached old Mr. Yusuf's cottage safely. The old man opened the door and his tired face broke into a wide, grateful smile when he saw the warm glow of the lantern. "You found the forest path at night!" he said, amazed. "The lantern helped me," said Aslan, holding Lumi up proudly. Lumi's flame burned bright and steady — not trembling at all now. On the walk back home, Lumi was quiet for a while. Then the small voice spoke again, softer and warmer than before. "Aslan, I think I understand now. The darkness was never the real problem. The real problem was that I forgot I had something to give. When you reminded me that my light was helping you, helping the path, helping Mr. Yusuf… I was not afraid anymore." Aslan nodded slowly. "And that," he said, "is the most important lesson I have ever learned too. When we focus on giving our light to others, we stop being afraid of the dark." That night, Lumi hung by Aslan's window and lit up the room with a warm, steady, peaceful glow — the glow of a lantern that had finally, truly learned to be brave.

🏮

You are a brave light, Aslan! 🌟

Just like Lumi discovered, courage is not the absence of fear — it is choosing to shine anyway. Every single person has a light inside them. Sometimes fear makes that light feel very small. But even a small, trembling flame can light up an entire dark path for someone who needs it. Your kindness, your effort, your smile — these are your light. Don't hide them. ✨🏮

💪 Today's Challenge: Think of one person in your life who might need some light today — a friend, a family member, a classmate. Do one small kind thing for them and notice how your own light grows brighter too! 🏮💛
