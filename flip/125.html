Street Food Around the World
📖 Teks deskriptif-komparatif tentang kuliner jalanan dunia 🔤 Descriptive + Comparative Writing 📚 Comparatives, Superlatives & Relative Clauses
Bacaan

Imagine walking down a bustling street and suddenly the air fills with the most incredible smells — something sizzling on a hot grill, something sweet melting on a warm pan, something spicy that makes your eyes water just a little. Welcome to the world of street food — one of the most exciting, authentic, and affordable ways to experience a new culture.

Street food is food that is prepared and sold by vendors in public places — on busy roads, in markets, outside train stations, and along beach promenades. Unlike restaurants, street food stalls are open to everyone. A student with little money and a famous chef can stand side by side at the same cart, eating the same delicious thing. That is what makes street food so democratic and special.

In Thailand, the streets of Bangkok are famous for pad thai — a stir-fried noodle dish with egg, tofu or shrimp, crushed peanuts, and a squeeze of lime. The aroma hits you from twenty metres away. In Mexico, you will find tacos al pastor — thin slices of marinated pork cooked on a rotating spit, served in soft tortillas with pineapple and salsa. In Japan, takoyaki — crispy balls of batter filled with octopus — are cooked in special round moulds right in front of you, and they are piping hot when they reach your hand.

In India, the streets are alive with the smell of chaat — a tangy, crispy, sweet, and spicy snack that is almost impossible to describe in words. You simply have to eat it. And in Turkey, simit — sesame-covered bread rings — are carried through the streets on trays balanced on vendors' heads, just as they have been for hundreds of years.

But what about Indonesia? Indonesia has some of the most diverse street food in the entire world. From siomay and batagor in Bandung — which is Aslan's hometown — to sate ayam, mie goreng, martabak, and es teler, every corner tells a different culinary story. In fact, many food experts argue that Indonesian street food is among the finest in Asia.

Street food is more than just eating — it is a window into a country's history, its ingredients, its daily rhythm, and the warmth of its people. The next time you stop at a gerobak on the side of the road in Bandung, remember: you are part of a tradition that is shared by billions of people all around the world. 🌍

Grammar Fokus
✦ Comparatives, Superlatives & Relative Clauses — Membandingkan dengan Tepat
Street food is more affordable than restaurants.
→ Comparative: more + adjective + than (lebih ... dari)
Indonesia has some of the most diverse street food in the world.
→ Superlative: the most + adjective (yang paling ...)
Chaat is a snack that is almost impossible to describe.
→ Relative clause dengan "that" — menerangkan kata benda sebelumnya
Simit is a tradition which has existed for hundreds of years.
→ Relative clause dengan "which" — untuk benda (bukan orang)
Vendors who sell batagor are everywhere in Bandung.
→ Relative clause dengan "who" — untuk orang / pedagang
💡 Comparatives vs Superlatives — Kapan pakai mana?
Bandingkan dua hal → pakai Comparative: Pad Thai is spicier than takoyaki.
Bandingkan satu hal dengan semua yang lain → pakai Superlative: Batagor is the tastiest street food in Bandung! 🏆

Relative clauses memungkinkanmu menggabungkan dua kalimat menjadi satu yang lebih kaya:
❌ "I love batagor. Batagor is from Bandung."  →  ✅ "I love batagor, which is from Bandung."
Percakapan
Daniel — Pen Pal Yogyakarta
Aslan! I just read your topic today — street food! Now I'm seriously hungry 😅. What's your absolute favourite street food from Bandung?
Aslan
Haha, that's the effect of talking about food! Mine is definitely batagor — it's fried tofu and fish dumplings with the most amazing peanut sauce, a drizzle of kecap manis, and a squeeze of lime. Every neighbourhood in Bandung has its own gerobak batagor and I think ours is the best 😄. What about Yogyakarta?
Daniel — Pen Pal Yogyakarta
Oh, Yogyakarta has amazing street food too! Gudeg is the most famous — young jackfruit cooked in coconut milk and palm sugar until it's very sweet and tender. And we have angkringan — little carts where you sit on bamboo benches and eat nasi kucing with all kinds of small dishes. It's so cosy!
Aslan
Angkringan sounds wonderful! I've seen it in photos. Sweet gudeg is so different from Bandung's food — our street food tends to be more savoury. That's what's incredible about Indonesia — every city has its own completely different street food culture. Bandung vs Yogyakarta vs Surabaya — it's like three different food worlds!
Daniel — Pen Pal Yogyakarta
Exactly! If you could eat street food from any other country in the world, which would you choose?
Aslan
Honestly? Japan — I would love to try takoyaki fresh from the mould. And Thai street food in Bangkok! The pad thai from a roadside wok with all the smoke and sizzle sounds incredible. But also, secretly, I think after tasting everything, I'd still come back to batagor. It's the most comforting food in the world — at least in my world 🍢😄.

Tulis Food Review dalam Bahasa Inggris!
Hari ini, pikirkan makanan jalanan favoritmu dan tulis mini review dalam bahasa Inggris! Gunakan:

✏️ Deskripsi sensorik: it smells like... / it tastes... / the texture is...
✏️ Comparatives: it is spicier than... / it is better than...
✏️ Relative clause: batagor, which is made from tofu, is my favourite...

Mulai dengan: "My favourite street food is ____. It is a dish which..."
