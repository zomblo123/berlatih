Chapter One
💌 The Golden Invitation
✅ Read! One autumn evening, Aslan found something unusual on his doorstep. It was a small envelope made of golden paper, tied with a thin orange ribbon. His name was written on the front in beautiful, curling letters that seemed to glow faintly in the dark. Aslan sat down on the front step and opened it carefully. Inside was a single card with just a few words: "You are invited to the Festival of Lights. Come to the great meadow tonight, when the moon is at its highest." Below the words was a small drawing of a lantern with a flame inside it. Aslan felt a warm flutter of excitement in his chest. He had heard of the Festival of Lights before — his grandfather used to tell stories about it. But Aslan had never gone. Tonight, that was going to change.

Chapter Two
🌙 The Night of Lights
✅ Read! When the moon climbed high and full into the sky, Aslan wrapped himself in his warmest coat and walked to the great meadow at the edge of town. He could see the glow from far away — hundreds of warm lights shimmering in the darkness. As he got closer, he gasped. The meadow was filled with people of all ages. Families sat together on blankets. Children ran between glowing paper lanterns that stood in rows on the grass. Everywhere Aslan looked, there were colours — deep gold, soft orange, rose pink, and violet — all flickering gently in the evening breeze. A tall woman with silver hair smiled at Aslan and handed him a round paper lantern with a short candle inside. "Welcome," she said warmly. "Hold it gently. Tonight, this lantern is yours." Aslan held the lantern with both hands and felt its gentle warmth against his fingers.

Chapter Three
🏮 Lanterns That Fly
✅ Read! A little while later, the silver-haired woman stood at the centre of the meadow and called out in a clear, ringing voice. "It is time! Light your sky lanterns and let them go!" Aslan looked around and saw that everyone now held a larger, lighter lantern — one made from thin paper with a small fire basket at the bottom. The woman next to Aslan helped him light the basket. The warm air rose inside the lantern and it began to lift slowly in his hands. "Now let go," the woman said softly. Aslan opened his hands. The lantern drifted upward, gently and silently, joining hundreds of others already climbing into the dark sky. Aslan watched, barely breathing. The sky above him filled with a rising constellation of gold, orange, pink, and purple lights — beautiful beyond anything he had words to describe. A few tears came to his eyes, but they were happy ones.

Chapter Four
🌠 Making a Wish
✅ Read! A young boy appeared beside Aslan, holding an unlit lantern. He looked nervous. "I don't know what to wish for," he said quietly. Aslan looked at him and smiled. "What do you love most?" Aslan asked. The boy thought for a moment. "I love my little sister. She has been sick for a long time. I wish she would get better." Aslan helped the boy light his lantern. Together they held it until it lifted. The boy let go and whispered his wish as the lantern rose. Aslan watched it join the others. Then he thought of his own wish. He did not wish for something for himself. Instead, he whispered, "I hope that everyone here tonight finds comfort and joy." At that moment, the star above his head seemed to shine just a little brighter — as if it had heard him.

Chapter Five
🌟 The Brightest Light Inside Us
✅ Read! As the night grew late, the lanterns faded one by one into the far distance until the sky was quiet again — though it felt warmer somehow, as if the lights had left something behind. The silver-haired woman walked to Aslan and sat beside him on the grass. "Did you enjoy the festival?" she asked. "It was the most beautiful thing I have ever seen," Aslan said honestly. She nodded slowly. "Every lantern carries a wish or a hope from the person who released it," she said. "But do you know the secret of this festival?" Aslan shook his head. "The lanterns do not grant the wishes," she continued gently. "They remind us. When we send a wish into the sky, we commit to working toward it. The real light — the one that makes things happen — is not in the lantern. It is inside each one of us." Aslan looked up at the now-dark sky. He understood. The festival was not about the lights in the sky. It was about the light each person carries inside their own heart. That was the light that never faded. That was the light worth protecting.
