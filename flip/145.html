The Chocolate River
Adventure

Chapter One
🌟 The Sweet Discovery
✅ Read! One sunny afternoon, Aslan was playing in the garden behind his house. He found a small, shiny door hidden behind a big rose bush. The door was the color of dark chocolate and smelled incredibly sweet. Aslan had never seen this door before. He looked around to make sure nobody was watching, then he knocked three times. The door opened by itself! A warm, sweet breeze blew out from inside. Aslan stepped through the tiny door and found himself in a land he had never imagined. The sky was pink and orange, like a giant piece of candy. The trees were made of white chocolate and their leaves were flat round lollipops in every color. The ground under his feet felt soft and a little sticky, like the surface of a caramel candy. Aslan's eyes went wide. He could not believe what he was seeing.

Chapter Two
🍫 The Chocolate River
✅ Read! Aslan walked along a path of pink sugar crystals until he heard a gentle gurgling sound. He followed the sound and arrived at the most amazing sight he had ever seen — a wide river flowing with thick, brown, warm chocolate! The river sparkled under the candy-colored sky. Aslan knelt down carefully and dipped one finger in. It was smooth and warm and tasted like the best hot chocolate he had ever drunk in his life. Little marshmallow boats floated gently down the river, carrying colorful candy flowers on top. On the other side of the river stood a row of tall candy cane trees — red and white and perfectly striped. A friendly-looking small creature sat by the riverbank. It had round green eyes and skin the color of a mint candy. "Hello!" it said with a big smile. "Welcome to the Land of Sweet. My name is Bubbles. Are you hungry, Aslan?"

Chapter Three
🍬 Land of Candy
✅ Read! Bubbles took Aslan on a tour of the Land of Sweet. Every single thing was made of something delicious and edible. The bridges over the chocolate river were made of dark chocolate bars, and their railings were long twisted licorice ropes. The houses in the village were built from giant cookies, with windows made of clear sugar and roofs covered in colorful sprinkles. Flowers made of gummy candy grew everywhere — red roses, yellow sunflowers, and purple violets, all chewy and sweet. Bubbles showed Aslan the famous Candy Mountain, where waterfalls of white chocolate poured down its rocky caramel slopes. There was also a forest of cotton candy trees, soft and fluffy and pink and blue. "Can I eat anything I want?" Aslan asked with shining eyes. "Yes!" said Bubbles happily. "Everything here grows back after you eat it. That is the magic of this place!" Aslan could not believe how wonderful this land was.

04
Chapter Four
🤢 Too Much Sugar
✅ Read! Aslan started eating. He ate a piece of the chocolate river bank. He chewed a gummy flower. He nibbled on a cookie house wall. He broke off a piece of a candy cane tree and sucked on it. He scooped up cotton candy from the trees by the handful. He drank three cups of chocolate river water. He ate a whole caramel rock from the mountain slopes. Bubbles watched nervously. "Aslan, maybe you should slow down a little," Bubbles said gently. But Aslan was having too much fun. He kept eating and eating. Then, after a while, Aslan stopped. His stomach felt heavy and uncomfortable. His head felt a little dizzy. He sat down on a soft marshmallow rock and put both hands on his tummy. "Oh no," he said slowly. "I think I ate too much." Everything around him still looked beautiful, but now the smell of so much sugar made him feel a bit sick. Bubbles sat beside him quietly and handed him a small cup of fresh water.

05
Chapter Five
💙 The Sweetest Lesson
✅ Read! After resting and drinking the fresh water, Aslan began to feel better. He looked around the beautiful candy land and thought carefully. "Bubbles," he said, "I was being greedy. I wanted to eat everything at once, and I did not enjoy any of it properly. I am sorry." Bubbles smiled kindly. "That is okay, Aslan. This happens to every visitor. The Land of Sweet teaches the same lesson every time. Too much of a wonderful thing stops being wonderful." Aslan nodded slowly. He thought about how the first bite of chocolate river had been magical — but after ten bites, he had barely noticed the taste. "I think I understand," said Aslan. "Good things are better when we appreciate them and do not take too much for ourselves. And maybe," he added with a smile, "they are even better when we share them with the people we love." Bubbles clapped two tiny green hands together with joy. Aslan picked one beautiful gummy flower and put it carefully in his pocket to bring home to his mother. Then he walked back through the chocolate-colored door, full of something sweeter than candy — wisdom and gratitude.
