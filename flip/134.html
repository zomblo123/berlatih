Chapter One
🏚️ The Old Bicycle in the Shed
✅ Read! Aslan was ten years old and loved to explore. One rainy afternoon, he found a rusty old key in a kitchen drawer. It was long and silver with a small star on top. Aslan did not know which door it opened. He walked around the house until he found a small wooden shed at the back of the garden. He had never opened it before. The key fit perfectly. Inside the shed, covered in dust and cobwebs, was an old bicycle. It was painted dark blue with golden stars on the frame. The tires were flat and the handlebars were rusty. But when Aslan touched it, the bicycle began to glow with a warm, soft light. Aslan's heart jumped. "This is no ordinary bicycle," he whispered.

02
Chapter Two
🚲 The First Flight
✅ Read! Aslan carefully wheeled the bicycle out of the shed. As soon as it touched the garden grass, all the rust disappeared. The tires became full and round. The frame shone like new. Aslan climbed onto the seat and held the handlebars. He started to pedal slowly. The bicycle moved forward — and then, suddenly, it rose into the air! Aslan gasped. He was flying! The garden below him grew smaller and smaller. The cold night wind blew through his hair. Aslan felt terrified for a moment, but then he laughed out loud. "I am flying!" he shouted. The bicycle climbed higher and higher into the dark sky. Below, the lights of his town twinkled like tiny golden dots. Aslan took a deep breath and smiled. He was not scared anymore. He was free.

03
Chapter Three
☁️ Above the Clouds
✅ Read! Aslan pedaled higher until he flew into a thick, white cloud. Everything went soft and quiet. The cloud felt cool and wet on his face, like a gentle mist. Then he came out on the other side. What he saw took his breath away. Above the clouds, the sky was perfectly clear. The moon was huge and bright. Millions of stars surrounded him like friends. Aslan reached out his hand and touched a star. It was warm, like a small candle. A gentle voice came from the star: "You are welcome here, brave rider." Aslan felt proud and peaceful. He rode slowly along the top of the clouds as if they were a white road. An owl flew beside him and hooted softly. "Even owls fly up here," Aslan laughed. He never wanted to come down.

mist candle peaceful surrounded gentle hooted brave
04
Chapter Four
🌃 The City of Lights
✅ Read! Aslan steered the bicycle downward and flew over a great city. The streets were full of golden lights. Tall buildings reached up like shining towers. Cars moved like tiny beetles far below. Aslan could see parks, bridges, and a wide river that reflected the city lights like a mirror. A bridge lit up with rainbow colors as he flew over it. People on top of a tall building were looking at the stars through a telescope. One child saw Aslan and pointed. "A flying bicycle!" the child called out. The people below waved, and Aslan waved back. He felt like a superhero. He swooped down low over the river and then pulled up high again, laughing all the way. "I can go anywhere," he thought. "The whole world is open to me." The night felt full of possibility.
🔑 Key Words — tap to hear
steered reflected possibility telescope swooped bridges beetles
05
Chapter Five
🌟 The Best Landing
✅ Read! After a long and wonderful flight, Aslan began to feel tired. The sky was turning from black to deep purple. Morning was coming. The bicycle turned on its own and pointed back toward home. Aslan understood. It was time to return. He guided the bicycle gently down through the clouds and back over his town. The streets were quiet and empty. Cats sat on fences. A baker was putting fresh bread in his window. The garden below looked warm and familiar. Aslan landed softly on the grass. The bicycle gave one last gentle glow, then became old and rusty again. Aslan pushed it back into the shed and locked the door. He went inside and sat by the window. Outside, the first birds of morning were singing. Aslan smiled quietly. He knew something now that not many people know: courage is not the absence of fear — it is choosing to fly anyway. And home? Home is always the best place to land.
