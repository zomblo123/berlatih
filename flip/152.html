The Snow Queen's Garden

A story about a boy named Aslan who discovers a magical garden of snow and ice — where everything is beautiful but cold, and where one warm heart can change everything. For Aslan (level: late beginner). ❄️🌸

Chapter One
🚪 The Frozen Door
✅ Read! One winter morning, Aslan was walking in the forest near his home. The air was cold and the trees were bare. Snow fell gently from the grey sky. Aslan pulled his red scarf tighter around his neck. Then he saw something very strange. Between two tall pine trees, there was a door made entirely of ice. It was big and beautiful, with patterns of snowflakes carved all over it. Cold white mist drifted from the edges. There was no handle, only a small mark shaped like a snowflake in the center. Aslan reached out and pressed his hand against it. The door felt freezing, but then something magical happened. The snowflake mark began to glow silver. With a deep creak, the door slowly opened. A wave of cold air rushed past him. Aslan stepped inside.

Chapter Two
🏔️ Entering the Snow Garden
✅ Read! Aslan gasped. Before him was the most breathtaking place he had ever seen. Everything was made of ice and snow. The trees were tall white crystals that sparkled in a soft blue light. The ground was covered in deep, perfect snow that crunched softly under his boots. Flowers made of ice stood in rows, each one glittering like tiny stars. In the distance, a great palace of ice rose up against a dark blue sky filled with bright stars. It was like a dream. But the garden was completely silent. No birds sang. No wind blew. No animals moved. Aslan could see his own breath in the cold air. It was beautiful, but also very lonely. He whispered to himself, "Someone lives here. But where are they?"

Chapter Three
👑 Meeting the Snow Queen
✅ Read! Aslan walked toward the ice palace. The great doors opened as he came near. Inside, the walls were made of pure, transparent ice. He could see stars through them. Sitting on a tall throne of ice was a woman. She wore a long white dress and a silver crown. Her hair was like fresh snow. Her eyes were pale blue, like the sky on a cold winter morning. She was the Snow Queen. She looked at Aslan with cold, distant eyes. "Why have you come?" she asked. Her voice was like wind through frozen branches. "I found your door," said Aslan. "I was curious." The Snow Queen stared at him for a long moment. Then she said very quietly, "No one has entered my garden for a hundred years." Aslan looked at her carefully. Behind her cold face, he thought he could see something — something like sadness.

Chapter Four
🌸 The Icy Flower
✅ Read! Aslan walked back out to the garden. He knelt down next to a beautiful ice flower. It was shaped like a rose, but made of clear, hard ice. He reached into his coat pocket and took out a small ordinary flower — a little yellow dandelion he had picked that morning before his walk. It was still warm from being in his pocket. He gently placed it next to the ice flower. Something extraordinary happened. Warmth spread from the dandelion. The ice around it began to melt slowly. The ice flower did not disappear — instead, its petals turned from clear ice to soft pink, like a real flower coming back to life. The Snow Queen appeared behind him. She did not speak. But Aslan heard a small sound — like a breath, or perhaps the beginning of a sob. She was crying. He had never seen a queen cry before.

Chapter Five
🌟 Melting the Cold Heart
✅ Read! Aslan stood up and turned to the Snow Queen. "Are you lonely?" he asked gently. The queen looked away. "I am a queen," she said. "Queens do not get lonely." But Aslan could see the truth in her eyes. "I get lonely too sometimes," he said. "But my mother says that loneliness melts when you let someone in." The Snow Queen was very still. Then, slowly, she looked at him. Her cold eyes became a little warmer. "I have been here alone for a very long time," she said, her voice barely a whisper. "I forgot what kindness felt like." Aslan smiled at her. "I can be your friend," he said. "If you let me visit." The queen looked at the pink flower, still blooming in the snow. Then she looked at Aslan. For the first time in a hundred years, she smiled. It was a small smile, but it was real and warm. All around them, more ice flowers slowly began to bloom with color. Aslan walked home that evening with a full heart. He had learned that even the coldest places can be warmed — by kindness, by friendship, and by one small act of love.
