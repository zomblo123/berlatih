Why Do We Dream?
📖 Teks informatif — sains yang diceritakan dengan cara menarik 🔤 Expository Writing 📚 Modal Verbs + Passive Voice
Bacaan

Every night, when we close our eyes and fall asleep, something remarkable happens inside our minds. We enter a world of dreams — sometimes vivid and exciting, sometimes strange and confusing, and sometimes so beautiful that we wish we could stay there forever. But why do we dream? Scientists have been asking this question for hundreds of years, and even today, the answer is not completely certain.

One popular theory is that dreaming helps us process our emotions and memories. During the day, we experience many things — happiness, stress, excitement, fear. While we sleep, our brains sort through all of these experiences, like a librarian organising books on a shelf. Dreams may be the brain's way of deciding which memories to keep and which to forget.

Another theory suggests that dreams help us prepare for real-life challenges. Imagine you are nervous about a school presentation. You might dream about standing in front of the class, speaking confidently — and wake up feeling a little less afraid. Some scientists believe that our sleeping minds practise difficult situations so that we feel more ready when they happen in real life.

There is also a fascinating stage of sleep called REM — which stands for Rapid Eye Movement. During REM sleep, your eyes move quickly back and forth beneath your eyelids, your heart beats faster, and your brain becomes almost as active as it is when you are awake. This is when most vivid dreams occur. Scientists can actually watch this stage happen in a sleep laboratory using special equipment.

Some dreams feel so real that, when we wake up, we are confused for a moment about what is true. Some people can even control their dreams — this is called lucid dreaming. In a lucid dream, you know that you are dreaming, so you can choose to fly, visit any place in the world, or talk to anyone you want — all inside your own imagination.

As for Aslan — he often dreams about adventure. Exploring deep jungles, building cities in the sky, and sometimes playing football with characters from his favourite manga. Whatever the science says, one thing is certain: dreams make the night magical.

Fakta Sains
REM Sleep — Saat Mimpi Terjadi
Rapid Eye Movement: mata bergerak cepat, jantung berdenyut lebih kencang, otak hampir seaktif saat terjaga.
Most vivid dreams occur during REM sleep.
✈️
Fenomena Khusus

Kamu tahu bahwa kamu sedang bermimpi — sehingga kamu bisa mengendalikan ceritanya! Terbang, berpetualang, bertemu siapa saja.
In a lucid dream, you can control what happens!

Grammar Fokus
✦ Modal Verbs + Passive Voice — Bahasa Sains yang Tepat
Dreams may be the brain's way of deciding.
→ "may be" = mungkin adalah (modal: kemungkinan)
You might dream about standing in front of class.
→ "might" = mungkin saja (lebih lemah dari "may")
Dreams can be controlled during lucid dreaming.
→ Passive Voice: subjek menerima aksi, bukan melakukan
This stage can be watched in a sleep lab.
→ can + be + past participle = bisa diamati / bisa dilakukan
Scientists have been asking this for hundreds of years.
→ Present Perfect Continuous: sudah bertanya sejak lama, sampai sekarang
💡 Mengapa Passive Voice sering dipakai di teks sains?
Karena fokusnya pada apa yang terjadi, bukan siapa yang melakukannya.
❌ "Scientists observe REM sleep."  →  ✅ "REM sleep is observed by scientists."
❌ "We don't know for sure."  →  ✅ "The answer is not completely certain."

Modal verbs untuk teori/kemungkinan: may · might · could · should · must — pilih sesuai seberapa yakin kamu!

Ceritakan Mimpimu dalam Bahasa Inggris!
Besok pagi, sebelum kamu benar-benar bangun, coba ingat mimpimu. Lalu tuliskan dalam satu paragraf bahasa Inggris. Gunakan:

✏️ Past tense: I dreamed that... / I was... / There was...
✏️ Deskripsi vivid: it felt... / the colours were... / I could smell...
✏️ Modal verbs: it might have been... / it could mean...

Mulai dengan: "Last night, I had a dream about..."
