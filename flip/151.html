Chapter One
🗺️ The Old Treasure Map
✅ Read! One sunny morning, Aslan was helping his uncle clean out an old storage room near the harbor. There were dusty boxes, broken fishing nets, and old rope everywhere. While moving a heavy wooden chest, Aslan noticed something tucked underneath it — a rolled-up piece of paper tied with a piece of red string. He carefully unrolled it. It was a map. The paper was brown and old, with the edges torn and soft from age. There were lines showing the ocean, small drawings of islands, and dotted paths crossing the sea. In the bottom corner, someone had drawn a large red X and written in old, faded ink: "The Hidden Island — where the real treasure waits." Aslan's heart jumped. He looked at his uncle. "Can I borrow a small boat?" he asked. His uncle laughed, but his eyes were curious.

Chapter Two
⛵ Sailing to the Hidden Island
✅ Read! His uncle agreed — as long as Aslan stayed safe and followed the map carefully. The next morning, Aslan set off in a small wooden boat with a white sail. He had packed his backpack with water, food, a compass, and the treasure map. The sea was calm and blue. The wind was gentle and steady. Aslan checked the map every few minutes, turning the boat left and right to follow the dotted path. After two hours of sailing, a dark green shape appeared on the horizon. As he got closer, Aslan saw tall palm trees, white sandy beaches, and a thick jungle beyond. There was no other boat, no houses, and no people that he could see. But as he stepped onto the beach and pulled the boat onto the sand, he heard a loud, friendly voice shout from the jungle: "AHOY! A visitor! Come in!"

Chapter Three
🏴‍☠️ Meeting the Friendly Pirates
✅ Read! Out of the jungle came a big, cheerful woman with curly red hair, a wide hat, and a warm smile. Behind her walked four more people — all different ages, all smiling. "Welcome to Coconut Island!" the woman said. "I am Captain Rosa. These are my crew." Aslan introduced himself. He showed Captain Rosa the map he had found. She recognized it immediately and laughed. "That old map! My grandfather drew it seventy years ago. He hid it in a storage room near the harbor, hoping the right person would find it one day." She put her hand on Aslan's shoulder. "I think that person is you." Aslan looked around at the crew. They were not scary at all. They were kind, funny, and full of stories. They gave Aslan a coconut drink and a seat by their fire. "So," said Captain Rosa, leaning forward with sparkling eyes, "shall we show you where the treasure is?"

Chapter Four
🕯️ The Secret Cave
✅ Read! Captain Rosa led Aslan and the crew through the jungle, past tall trees and colourful birds. They came to a rocky hill at the center of the island. Hidden behind a large waterfall was a dark opening. "The cave," said Captain Rosa. They all ducked under the waterfall and went inside. The cave was beautiful. The walls sparkled with tiny crystals that caught the light. Water dripped somewhere in the darkness, making a soft, musical sound. Deep inside, there was a large wooden chest with iron handles. Aslan helped the crew push away the heavy stone in front of it. Everyone held their breath. Captain Rosa lifted the lid. Inside were dozens of old gold coins, colorful jewels, old letters, drawings, and photographs of people from long ago. "This is our family's history," said Captain Rosa softly. "That is what grandfather called the real treasure — the story of who we are."

05
Chapter Five
🌟 The Real Treasure
✅ Read! Aslan and the crew spent the rest of the day on the island. They cooked fish over the fire, told stories, laughed, and explored the beach together. Captain Rosa gave Aslan one gold coin from the chest as a gift. "So you always remember your first real adventure," she said. When it was time to go, the whole crew walked Aslan back to his boat. They helped push it into the water and waved from the beach as he sailed away. On the way home, Aslan thought about what had happened. He had gone looking for gold. But what he found was much richer: new friends, a story about family, the courage to sail alone, and the joy of working together. Back at the harbor, his uncle was waiting. Aslan held up the gold coin. "I found the treasure," he said. His uncle smiled. "Tell me everything," he said. And Aslan did — all the way home.

Ahoy, Aslan! You found the treasure! ⚓🌟
