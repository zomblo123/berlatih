A Problem at School
📖 Menceritakan konflik, perasaan, dan resolusi secara terstruktur 🔤 Problem–Solution Narrative (B1) 📚 Reported Speech + Emotion Vocabulary
Bacaan

It started as an ordinary Monday morning. I arrived at school early, unpacked my bag, and was chatting with Fathan when Bu Nita — our Science teacher — announced that she would be returning our group project scores. My stomach dropped. Our group had worked hard on that project. Surely it would be fine.

It was not fine. Our group received a score of 62 — well below what I had expected. Worse, Reza — a boy who sits in the front row and always seems to know everything — turned around and said loudly, "62? That's embarrassing." Several classmates laughed. My face went hot with embarrassment, and I felt a sharp sting of anger rising in my chest.

During break time, I told Fathan what I was feeling. He listened carefully and then said, "You should talk to Bu Nita. Maybe there was a mistake, or at least you can understand what went wrong." I wasn't sure. I was still upset and part of me just wanted to forget the whole thing. But Fathan was right — avoiding the problem wouldn't make it disappear.

After the next lesson, I approached Bu Nita's desk. My hands were slightly trembling. "Excuse me, Bu Nita," I said. "Could I ask about our project score? I'm not complaining — I just want to understand what we could improve." She looked at me and smiled warmly. "Of course, Aslan. I'm glad you asked."

She explained that our content was good, but our presentation lacked structure and our conclusion was too short. She also said that she could see we had put in effort, and she offered us a chance to submit a revised version for a better score. I thanked her and walked back to my seat feeling much lighter.

As for Reza — I decided not to argue with him. Instead, I told myself: next time, our work would speak for itself. When I got home, I immediately opened my laptop and started rewriting the conclusion. Ibu Fety — who heard the whole story at dinner — said she was proud of me for handling it maturely. That meant more to me than any score.

Grammar Fokus
✦ Reported Speech — Menceritakan Apa yang Dikatakan Orang Lain
Direct: "62? That's embarrassing."
→ Ini kata-kata asli Reza — langsung dikutip, pakai tanda petik
Reported: He said that it was embarrassing.
→ Reported Speech: kata kerja mundur satu tense (is → was). "That" bisa dipakai atau dihilangkan.
She explained that our content was good.
→ said / told / explained / mentioned / replied + that + kalimat (tense mundur)
She offered us a chance
to submit a revised version.
→ offer + someone + noun/infinitive — tidak perlu "that" untuk pola ini
Fathan said I should talk to Bu Nita.
→ "should" tidak berubah dalam Reported Speech — sudah bentuk modal past
💡 Kata kerja laporan (reporting verbs) yang sering dipakai:
said / told (someone) / explained / mentioned / replied / suggested / warned / promised / offered

Perubahan tense utama: is → was · are → were · will → would · can → could · has → had

✏️ Latih: ubah kalimat ini → Direct: "I'm proud of you."
Reported: Ibu Fety said that she was proud of me. ✅

Tips Belajar Hari Ini
😤
Ceritakan Masalah dan Cara Mengatasinya!
Tulis satu paragraf tentang masalah yang pernah kamu hadapi — di sekolah, di rumah, atau di mana saja. Sertakan:

✏️ Masalah (Problem): Apa yang terjadi? Bagaimana perasaanmu? Gunakan emotion vocabulary!
✏️ Konflik (Conflict): Apa yang membuat situasinya sulit?
✏️ Resolusi (Resolution): Apa yang kamu lakukan? Apa hasilnya?
✏️ Gunakan Reported Speech: "Fathan said that...", "She explained that...", "He told me..."

Mulai dengan: "It started as an ordinary [day/morning/afternoon] when..."

Ingat Aslan: menghadapi masalah dengan kepala dingin adalah tanda kedewasaan yang sesungguhnya! 💪
