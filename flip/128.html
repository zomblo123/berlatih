Making Decisions
📖 Memahami proses berpikir dan mengungkapkan pilihan dengan percaya diri 🔤 Reflective Narrative 📚 Conditional Sentences + Expressing Choice
Bacaan

Every single day, from the moment I open my eyes, I am making decisions. Some are so small I barely notice — which shirt to wear, whether to eat rice or bread, which YouTube video to watch first. But some decisions are bigger, and those are the ones that shape who I am becoming.

A few weeks ago, I faced one of those bigger decisions. My friend Fathan asked me to join a new football team that trains every Saturday morning. At exactly the same time, Ibu Fety had been encouraging me to use my Saturday mornings to practise English and read. I felt torn — I love football deeply, but I also know how much my English has been improving, and I did not want to lose that momentum.

I decided to do what Ayah Sigit always recommends: make a list. On one side, I wrote all the reasons to join the football team — the fun, the exercise, the teamwork, spending time with Fathan. On the other side, I wrote all the reasons to protect my English practice time — the progress I had made, my goal to speak English fluently one day, the fact that Ibu Fety had already helped me so much.

When I looked at both sides, I realised something. This did not have to be an either-or choice. I could join the football team for the matches on Saturdays, and still keep my quiet reading time on Sunday mornings. I talked to Fathan, who immediately agreed. I talked to Ibu Fety, who smiled and said, "That is exactly the kind of creative thinking that good decision-makers use, Aslan."

Good decision-making, I have learned, is not always about choosing between two things. Sometimes the best answer is to ask: "How can I have both?" or "What is the most important thing here?" or simply, "What would I regret not doing?"

Kakek Cecep once told me: "A good decision is not always the easiest one. But a brave decision, made with a clear heart, will always lead you somewhere worth going." I think about those words a lot. And I think about them every time I open this flipbook, too — because choosing to learn, every single day, is one of the best decisions I have ever made.

5 Langkah Membuat Keputusan yang Baik
1
Langkah Pertama
Kenali Masalahnya dengan Jelas
Sebelum memutuskan apapun, pastikan kamu benar-benar mengerti apa pilihan yang ada. Jangan terburu-buru! Tanya diri sendiri: "Apa sebenarnya yang harus aku putuskan?"
What exactly do I need to decide?
2
Langkah Kedua
Kumpulkan Informasi
Cari tahu semua yang perlu kamu ketahui. Bertanya kepada orang yang lebih berpengalaman — seperti Ayah, Ibu, atau Kakek — bisa sangat membantu di langkah ini.
Let me think about this carefully first.
3
Langkah Ketiga
Timbang Sisi Baik dan Buruknya
Tulis daftar pro (keuntungan) dan cons (kerugian) untuk setiap pilihan. Aslan melakukan ini saat memutuskan antara tim sepak bola dan waktu belajar Inggris!
On one hand... but on the other hand...
4
Langkah Keempat
Pikirkan Konsekuensinya
Bayangkan apa yang akan terjadi setelah keputusanmu — minggu depan, bulan depan, tahun depan. Apakah kamu akan menyesal? Atau merasa bangga dengan pilihan ini?
If I choose this, then... / What would I regret?
5
Langkah Kelima
Putuskan dengan Berani & Bertanggung Jawab
Setelah berpikir matang, ambil keputusanmu! Dan yang paling penting — bertanggung jawab atas pilihanmu. Jika keputusanmu tidak sempurna, kamu bisa belajar darinya.
I've decided to... / I'm going with... because...

Ungkapan Membuat Keputusan dalam Bahasa Inggris
Saat meminta waktu
Let me think about it first.
Biar aku pikir dulu.
Saat menimbang pilihan
On one hand... but on the other hand...
Di satu sisi... tapi di sisi lain...
Saat sudah memutuskan
I've made up my mind. I'm going to...
Aku sudah memutuskan. Aku akan...
Saat menjelaskan alasan
I chose this because... / The main reason is...
Aku memilih ini karena... / Alasan utamanya adalah...
Saat mencari jalan tengah
Maybe we could find a compromise.
Mungkin kita bisa menemukan jalan tengah.
Saat meminta pendapat
What would you do in my position?
Apa yang akan kamu lakukan kalau jadi aku?
Saat ragu-ragu
I'm not sure yet. I need to weigh up the options.
Aku belum yakin. Aku perlu menimbang pilihannya.
Saat memikirkan masa depan
If I choose this, I think I'll feel... / What would I regret?
Kalau aku pilih ini, aku rasa aku akan... / Apa yang akan kusesali?
Grammar Fokus
✦ Conditional Sentences untuk Keputusan + Expressing Choice
If I join the team, I will lose study time.
→ First Conditional: If + present simple, will + verb — konsekuensi nyata yang mungkin terjadi
If I had more time, I would do both.
→ Second Conditional: If + past simple, would + verb — situasi khayalan / tidak nyata
I decided to find a compromise.
→ decide + to + verb = memutuskan untuk melakukan sesuatu
I chose football over extra study.
→ choose X over Y = memilih X daripada Y
It doesn't have to be either-or.
→ "doesn't have to be" = tidak harus — membuka kemungkinan ketiga!
What would you do in my position?
→ "would" dalam pertanyaan = meminta saran/opini secara sopan
💡 First vs Second Conditional — perbedaan penting:
✅ "If I join the team, I will miss English class." → nyata, mungkin terjadi
✅ "If I had wings, I would fly to London." → khayalan, tidak nyata sekarang

Cara mudah ingat: First Conditional = situasi mungkin terjadi → pakai will. Second Conditional = situasi khayalan → pakai would.
Percakapan
Fathan — Sahabat Aslan
Aslan! Coach Wahyu wants to know — are you going to join our football team on Saturdays? We really want you on the team, man!
Aslan
Hey Fathan! I really want to — I love playing with you guys. But I need to think about it carefully first. I've been doing my English reading practice every Saturday morning and I don't want to lose that momentum. Can I give you an answer tomorrow?
Fathan — Sahabat Aslan
Of course! Take your time. But what's the problem exactly? Can't you just do both?
Aslan
That's what I was thinking! On one hand, football is great exercise and I love playing with the team. On the other hand, my English has been improving a lot and I really don't want to stop now. I've made up my mind — I'll join the matches on Saturdays, and keep my English study time on Sunday mornings. It doesn't have to be either-or!
Fathan — Sahabat Aslan
That's a brilliant idea! I should have thought of that. But wait — won't you be tired after football?
Aslan
Actually, Ibu Fety always says that a healthy body helps a healthy mind. If I exercise on Saturdays, I think I'll focus better on Sundays! And if I'm ever too tired, I can always adjust. The important thing is that I don't completely give up either one. This feels like the right decision.
Fathan — Sahabat Aslan
Aslan the philosopher! Haha. But seriously — welcome to the team! Coach Wahyu will be thrilled. And I'll personally make sure we celebrate every goal in English! ⚽
Aslan
Hahaha — deal! And when I score, I'll shout "MAGNIFICENT!" instead of "GOL!" just to confuse everyone. 😄 Thanks for being patient with me, Fathan. A good friend always gives you space to think.

Tips Belajar Hari Ini

Tulis Keputusan Terbaikmu dalam Bahasa Inggris!
Pikirkan satu keputusan yang pernah kamu buat yang kamu bangga — sekecil apapun. Lalu tulis 3–4 kalimat dalam bahasa Inggris:

✏️ Apa keputusannya? — "I decided to..."
✏️ Apa yang kamu pertimbangkan? — "On one hand... but on the other hand..."
✏️ Mengapa kamu memilih itu? — "I chose this because..."
✏️ Bagaimana perasaanmu sekarang? — "I'm glad I decided to..."

Contoh: "I decided to open this flipbook every day. On one hand, it took time. But on the other hand, my English has improved so much. I'm really glad I made that decision."
