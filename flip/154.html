Chapter One
☁️ The Floating Cloud
✅ Read! One clear morning, Aslan was lying on the soft green grass in his garden, staring up at the sky. The sky was a beautiful deep blue, and dozens of white clouds drifted slowly overhead. Aslan loved to watch the clouds and imagine shapes in them — a sleeping bear, a sailing ship, a friendly dragon. But one cloud was different. It was lower than the others, and it moved against the wind instead of with it. Aslan sat up and watched carefully. The cloud came closer and closer until it was floating just above the garden fence, soft and bright like a pile of fresh cotton. Then Aslan heard a tiny, silvery voice coming from inside the cloud. "We have been waiting for you, Aslan," the voice said gently. "The Cloud Kingdom is ready for its visitor."

Chapter Two
🌀 Climbing to the Sky
✅ Read! The cloud touched the top of the garden fence and held perfectly still. Aslan stood up slowly, his heart beating fast with excitement. He reached out his hand and touched the cloud. It felt cool and soft, like touching a fresh pillow. The silvery voice spoke again. "Step on. We will carry you safely." Aslan took a deep breath. He placed one foot on the cloud, then the other. The cloud held him firmly, as solid as a floor. Then, very gently, it began to rise. Aslan's garden grew smaller below him. The trees became tiny. The rooftops of his neighbourhood looked like a patchwork quilt of orange and red tiles. Higher and higher the cloud carried him, through a layer of cold mist that left tiny droplets on his hair. And then, suddenly, he broke through — and the Cloud Kingdom appeared above him in all its glowing, breathtaking glory.

Chapter Three
🏰 Welcome to Cloud Kingdom
✅ Read! The Cloud Kingdom was more wonderful than anything Aslan had ever imagined. The ground was made of thick, soft cloud — springy and white, like walking on the world's largest marshmallow. Everywhere Aslan looked, there were beautiful structures made entirely from cloud and mist. Tall towers rose into the blue sky, their peaks decorated with tiny rainbows. Wide bridges connected island clouds that floated at different heights. Rivers of mist flowed gently between them. Small creatures lived here — beings made entirely of cloud, soft and round and white, with dark little eyes full of kindness. One of them floated up to Aslan and bowed politely. "Welcome, Aslan," it said in a whisper-soft voice. "I am Nimbus. I will be your guide today." Nimbus had a round little body and two small cloud-puff arms. It smiled warmly, and Aslan immediately felt safe and welcome.

04
Chapter Four
🌈 The Cloud Palace
✅ Read! Nimbus led Aslan to the heart of the Cloud Kingdom — the Cloud Palace. Its walls shimmered with soft white light, and its towers were so tall they disappeared into the blue above. Inside, the palace was airy and open, with no heavy stone or dark wood. Everything was made of mist and light. The floors were smooth and cool under Aslan's feet. The walls glowed faintly like moonlight through fog. In the palace's great hall, Aslan saw the most extraordinary thing — a wide round window, like a giant eye looking down at the Earth below. He walked to it and peered through. The world he knew — his street, his school, his town — looked tiny and peaceful from up here, like a beautiful painting. Green fields, silver rivers, and tiny roads that wound through villages like ribbons. Aslan pressed his hand against the cool cloud-glass and felt his heart fill with something warm and quiet. He understood, for the first time, how big and beautiful the world truly was.

05
Chapter Five
🏠 Flying Back Home
✅ Read! After a long and magical afternoon, Nimbus came to Aslan and spoke softly. "The sun is beginning to descend," Nimbus said. "It is time for you to go home." Aslan felt a little sad, but he nodded. He had learned so much and seen so much beauty. The same kind cloud that had brought him up now waited at the edge of the kingdom, ready to carry him gently back down. As Aslan stepped on, all the cloud creatures gathered and waved their soft little arms. "Come back soon!" they called in their whispering voices. The cloud descended slowly, and Aslan watched the Cloud Kingdom grow smaller above him — the palace, the towers, the rainbow bridges — until they vanished into the mist. When his feet touched the grass of his garden again, everything looked different. Not because it had changed — but because he had. Aslan now saw his garden, his street, and his whole world with new eyes. He understood something important: sometimes you need to go very high to truly appreciate what is below. The beauty of the world was not only up in the clouds. It was right here, at home, all along.
