Chapter One
🕰️ The Old Grandfather Clock
✅ Read! One rainy Saturday, Aslan was helping his grandmother clean her old house. Most of the rooms were tidy, but there was one room at the end of the hallway that was always kept locked. That day, his grandmother handed him a heavy iron key. "I think it is time you saw what is inside," she said with a mysterious smile. Aslan turned the key and pushed the door open. The room smelled of dust and old wood. In the center stood a tall grandfather clock made of dark brown wood with carvings of leaves and stars. The clock was silent. Its hands had not moved in many years. But as soon as Aslan stepped closer, the clock face began to glow with a soft golden light.

Chapter Two
✨ The Magic Tick
✅ Read! Aslan reached out and touched the clock's golden pendulum. The moment his fingers touched the cold metal, the clock gave one loud tick. The golden glow grew brighter and filled the whole room. A voice came from inside the clock — deep and gentle, like the sound of an old bell. "You have been chosen," the voice said. "This clock has one special power. When you turn the hands backward, time will stop for everyone except you. Only one hour. Use it wisely." Aslan's heart was pounding. He looked at the clock hands and carefully turned them back by one hour. There was a flash of golden light. Then everything went still. He ran to the window. Outside, the rain had stopped in the air. Every drop was frozen like tiny glass beads.

Chapter Three
⏸️ Time Stops
✅ Read! Aslan stepped outside into the frozen world. It was the most extraordinary sight he had ever seen. A cat was frozen in mid-jump near the gate. A boy on a bicycle was frozen in the middle of the street, leaning to one side. Even a butterfly had stopped with its wings open wide. Aslan walked past them all, laughing with wonder. He ran to the bakery down the road. The baker was frozen with his arms raised, pulling a tray of hot bread from the oven. Aslan took one warm bread roll from the tray and left a coin on the counter as payment. He climbed to the top of the frozen fountain in the square and looked out at the perfectly still city. Not a sound. Not a breath. Just the golden glow of stopped time all around him.

Chapter Four
🌅 A Day Without Time
✅ Read! As Aslan explored the frozen city, a strange feeling slowly crept into his chest. At first it was fun — he could do anything without rushing. But after a while, everything began to feel lonely. There were no voices. No laughter. No footsteps. Even the birds were silent and still in the trees above him. Aslan sat on a bench and ate his bread roll alone. He missed the sound of his grandmother calling his name. He missed the noise of the busy street. He looked at his watch and realized the one hour was nearly over. He ran back to the old house as fast as he could. When he stepped back into the locked room, the golden glow of the clock was fading. He pressed his hand to the pendulum once more. With a soft chime, time began to move again.

Chapter Five
🌟 Learning to Cherish Time
✅ Read! The rain began to fall again. The cat finished its jump. The boy on the bicycle kept riding. The world moved on as if nothing had happened. Aslan walked back to his grandmother, who was in the kitchen making tea. "Did you enjoy the room?" she asked. "Yes," said Aslan quietly. He sat down and thought hard about what he had experienced. "Grandmother," he said, "the clock showed me something important. When time stopped, I was free to do anything — but it felt empty without the people I love." His grandmother smiled and poured him a warm cup of tea. "That is the secret of time, Aslan," she said. "It is not something to stop or waste. It is something to fill — with kindness, with effort, and with the people who matter to you." Aslan looked back toward the locked room. He understood now. He decided that from that day on, he would never procrastinate again. He would do his homework on time. He would call his friends. He would sit with his grandmother every evening — because time, once gone, never comes back.
