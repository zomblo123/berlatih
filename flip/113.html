On the Phone
📖 Percakapan telepon sederhana & frasa umum 🔤 Can I speak to...? 📚 Phone Phrases
Bacaan

Hello! I am Aslan. Today I want to talk about something we do every day — talking on the phone! In our family, the phone is very important. My father Sigit uses it for his business and his content creation. My mother Fety calls the school to discuss her students. And I use my phone to call Fathan when we need to talk about something important — like our Minecraft strategy!

Last Saturday, something interesting happened. My father Sigit was in his studio and he got a phone call from someone he did not know. I answered the phone for him. The caller said, "Hello, can I speak to Mr Sigit, please?" I said, "I'm sorry, he is busy right now. Can I take a message?" The caller said, "Yes, please tell him that the recording session is on Tuesday at ten o'clock." I wrote it down and gave it to Ayah Sigit. He was very proud of me!

Another time, I called Fathan to invite him to play football on Saturday. I said, "Hello, this is Aslan. Can I speak to Fathan, please?" His mother answered and said, "Hold on, please. I'll get him." Then Fathan came to the phone and we made our plan.

Sometimes I also call my mother Fety when I arrive home from school. I say, "Hi Mum, I'm home. Don't worry!" She always feels relieved when she hears my voice. My mother says that communication is the key to a happy family.

Knowing how to speak on the phone in English is a very useful skill. You can use it when calling a hotel, a restaurant, or even a friend in another country. It is a little different from normal conversation — you need to be very clear because the other person cannot see your face!

Frasa Penting saat Telepon
📞 Membuka percakapan
Hello! / Good morning!
Halo! / Selamat pagi!
🗣️ Memperkenalkan diri
This is Aslan (speaking).
Ini Aslan (yang berbicara).
🙋 Minta bicara dengan seseorang
Can I speak to Fathan, please?
Boleh saya bicara dengan Fathan?
⏳ Minta tunggu sebentar
Hold on / Just a moment, please.
Tunggu sebentar.
📝 Minta tinggalkan pesan
Can I take a message?
Boleh saya catat pesannya?
❓ Tanya siapa yang menelepon
May I ask who is calling?
Boleh tahu siapa yang menelepon?
😔 Orang yang dituju tidak ada
I'm afraid he/she is not available.
Maaf, beliau sedang tidak bisa.
👋 Mengakhiri percakapan
Thank you! Goodbye / Bye!
Terima kasih! Selamat tinggal!
🔄 Minta ulangi
Could you repeat that, please?
Bisakah diulangi?
📵 Sambungan buruk
Sorry, the line is not clear.
Maaf, suaranya tidak jelas.
💬 Meninggalkan pesan
Please tell him/her that...
Tolong sampaikan bahwa...
📞 Mengonfirmasi pesan
I will give him/her the message.
Saya akan menyampaikan pesannya.
Do's & Don'ts saat Telepon dalam Bahasa Inggris
✅ DO — Lakukan ini!
✓ Mulai dengan "Hello!" yang jelas
✓ Perkenalkan diri: "This is [nama]"
✓ Bicara pelan dan jelas
✓ Konfirmasi pesan: "Tuesday at ten, correct?"
✓ Akhiri dengan sopan: "Thank you, goodbye!"
✓ Gunakan "please" dan "thank you"
❌ DON'T — Hindari ini!
✗ Jangan langsung tanya tanpa salam
✗ Jangan bilang "I am Aslan" — pakai "This is"
✗ Jangan bicara terlalu cepat
✗ Jangan tutup telepon tanpa pamit
✗ Jangan lupa tulis pesan penting
✗ Jangan bilang "Who are you?" — pakai "May I ask who is calling?"

Grammar Fokus
✦ Phone Phrases — Pola Kalimat Penting saat Telepon
Can I speak to Fathan, please?
→ Cara sopan minta bicara dengan seseorang
This is Aslan (speaking).
→ Perkenalkan diri di telepon — BUKAN "I am Aslan"
May I ask who is calling?
→ Sopan menanyakan identitas penelepon
Could you repeat that, please?
→ Could you + kata kerja = permintaan sopan
I'm afraid he is not available.
→ "I'm afraid" = cara sopan menyampaikan kabar kurang baik
Please tell him that...
→ Menitipkan pesan: Please tell him/her + that + pesan
💡 Ingat: Saat memperkenalkan diri di telepon, selalu gunakan "This is [nama]" — bukan "I am [nama]". Ini adalah aturan standar dalam percakapan telepon bahasa Inggris!

✅ "This is Aslan."  ❌ "I am Aslan." (terdengar aneh di telepon)

Gunakan "Can I..." atau "Could I..." — keduanya benar, tapi Could lebih formal dan sopan.
Percakapan
Guru — Miss Dewi
Aslan, have you ever spoken English on the phone?
Aslan
Yes, Miss! Last Saturday someone called for my father Sigit. He was busy in his studio, so I answered. I said "Hello, I'm afraid he is busy right now. Can I take a message?" and the caller said yes! I wrote down the message and gave it to Ayah Sigit. He was really proud of me!
Guru — Miss Dewi
Excellent, Aslan! What was the most important phrase you used?
Aslan
"Can I take a message?" — that was the most useful one! And I also asked "May I ask who is calling?" because I needed to know their name to tell Ayah Sigit. The caller was Pak Andi from the recording studio.
Guru — Miss Dewi
Very good! What should you say when YOU call someone?
Aslan
First say "Hello!" then introduce yourself with "This is [your name]" — NOT "I am [your name]". Then say "Can I speak to [the person], please?" I practice this every time I call Fathan. His mum always says "Hold on please, I'll get him!" — and that's my favourite part because I know Fathan is coming!
Guru — Miss Dewi
Perfect! One last question — what do you say when the line is not clear?
Aslan
"Sorry, the line is not clear. Could you repeat that, please?" — I've had to say that a few times when calling from home. The signal is sometimes weak near our house. But Enin Ii says I always sound very polite on the phone! 😄
