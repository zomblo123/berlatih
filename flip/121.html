My Journey to the City
📖 Menceritakan perjalanan dengan ekspresi yang lebih kaya dan terstruktur 🔤 Personal Narrative (B1) 📚 Past Simple + Past Continuous + Connectives
Bacaan

It was a Sunday morning when Ayah Sigit announced something that made my heart jump: "Aslan, kita ke Bandung kota hari ini. Ada keperluan di studio rekaman." I had been to the city before, of course, but always with the whole family in our car. This time, Ayah said we would take the angkot — the local minibus — just the two of us, like real city people.

We departed early, while the morning air was still cool and crisp. The neighbourhood was barely waking up — a few cats stretched on walls, a warung owner was arranging her bread display, and the smell of freshly brewed coffee drifted from somewhere down the alley. I clutched my backpack — inside: a notebook, two pens, a banana, and a small bottle of water. I was ready for an adventure.

The angkot was already crowded when we climbed in. We squeezed onto the narrow bench next to a woman carrying a large basket of vegetables and an old man reading a folded newspaper. The engine rattled and hummed as we wound through the hilly roads of Bandung. Through the open window, I watched the city slowly unfold — mosques with tall minarets, colourful shopfronts, and schoolchildren on bicycles. It felt like watching a film.

When we arrived at the city centre, I was immediately struck by how different it felt from our quiet neighbourhood. The streets were buzzing with motorcycles, street vendors, and pedestrians. Ayah navigated through the crowd confidently while I tried to keep up, turning my head left and right so I wouldn't miss anything.

The recording studio was on the third floor of an old colonial building near Jalan Braga. While Ayah was busy with his work, his colleague, Pak Rendra, offered to show me around. We walked along Braga Street — one of the oldest streets in Bandung — and he pointed out beautiful art deco buildings that were built during the Dutch colonial era. "These buildings are more than a hundred years old," he said. I traced my fingers along the stone wall. History felt suddenly very real and very close.

On the way home, Ayah bought me a warm batagor from a street cart. We ate it standing on the pavement, watching the city rush past us. "Did you enjoy today?" Ayah asked. I nodded — more than I could say in words. The city had been loud, busy, and overwhelming — but also magnificent. I already knew I wanted to come back.

Grammar Fokus
✦ Past Simple vs Past Continuous — Latar Belakang vs Aksi Utama
The neighbourhood was waking up
when we departed.
→ Past Continuous (was/were + -ing) = aktivitas latar belakang yang sedang berlangsung saat kejadian utama terjadi
A warung owner was arranging
her bread display.
→ Menggambarkan suasana / apa yang sedang terjadi di sekitar = Past Continuous
I watched the city slowly unfold.
→ Past Simple = satu aksi tunggal yang selesai di masa lalu
While Ayah was working,
Pak Rendra offered to show me around.
→ "while + Past Continuous" diikuti Past Simple = pola klasik B1. Satu aksi berlangsung, aksi lain menyela atau bersamaan
The streets were buzzing with
motorcycles and vendors.
→ "buzzing" memberi kesan hidup — bukan sekedar "there were motorcycles", tapi kamu bisa merasakannya!
💡 Trik B1 — Gunakan keduanya! Past Simple untuk urutan kejadian: "We arrived, I looked around, Ayah bought batagor."
Past Continuous untuk suasana dan latar: "The city was buzzing, vendors were calling out, people were rushing past."

Connectives penting: while / when / as soon as / by the time / although / however / on the other hand — kata-kata ini yang membuat naratif B1 terasa lebih dewasa dari A2!

Tips Belajar Hari Ini
🏙️
Tulis Cerita Perjalananmu Sendiri!
Ceritakan satu perjalanan yang berkesan — ke pasar, ke sekolah baru, ke tempat wisata, atau ke rumah kerabat. Gunakan:

✏️ Past Continuous untuk suasana: "The market was buzzing with noise..."
✏️ Past Simple untuk urutan kejadian: "I arrived, looked around, and immediately..."
✏️ Connectives: while / as soon as / by the time / although / however
✏️ Detail sensorik: apa yang kamu lihat, dengar, cium, rasakan?

Mulai dengan: "It was a [day] when I first..." atau "I had been to [place] before, but never..."
